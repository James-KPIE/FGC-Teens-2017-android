2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 46

Make a Joyful Noise

Lesson Starts

Memory Verse: “Come, let us sing for joy to the Lord; let us shout aloud to the Rock of our salvation. Let us come before him with thanksgiving and extol him with music and song.” - Psalm 95:1-2

Bottom Line: We must always respond to the call to rejoice in the Lord at all times.

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson teens will learn to appreciate the significance of joy in Christian living.

Lesson Introduction

Our topic “make a joyful noise” is an imperative statement. It is not a conditional disposition. It didn’t say make a joyful noise when you feel good or when you hit a jackpot or whatever reason you may have to do it. Joy is an unconditional virtue. It is not something you feel because of, it is what you express in spite of. Joy is a fruit of the Spirit that defies surrounding situations. You don’t feel to rejoice, you choose to rejoice, because the Holy Spirit is inside of you and He fills your heart with joy. More importantly, to make a joyful noise is not a matter of feeling, it’s a matter of obedience. Paul said to the Philippians, Rejoice in the Lord always. I will say it again: Rejoice! (Phil. 4:4). So when the psalmist say, “Come, let us sing for joy to the Lord” (v.1), it is a bugle blast that must be obeyed.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

This Psalm without a title is though attributed to David was believed to have been composed during the time of the captivity. The author of the Hebrew epistle also in following the common opinion attributed the Psalm to David (cf. Heb 4:3-7).

The Psalm is a solemn invitation to the people to praise God from a sense of his great goodness; and to be attentive to divine instructions. The psalmist warned the nation of Israel not to repeat the folly of unbelief that cost its ancestors the promised rest in the land. This warning was prompted by the incidents at Rephidim where the people murmured, strove (Meribah) and tested (Massah) God (cf. Exo. 17; Num. 20:1-13, Psa. 81:7; 106:32). So God swore that they would not enter the land, but that they would perish in the wilderness, except for the younger the younger generation.

Lesson Outline

I. Rejoice In God’s Eminence (V.1-5)

II. Rejoice In God’s Benevolence (V.8-11).

III. Rejoice In God’s Providence (V.6-7).

Lesson Commentary

I. Rejoice in God’s Eminence

We rejoice because God is God. He is our God. He created us. He created us for His pleasure (Rev. 4:11). If His other creatures are showing forth His praises (Psa. 19:1-3), then we that He created after His likeness must do more. He is God, in all generations and for eternity. The psalmist writes, “Blessed are those who have learned to acclaim you, who walk in the light of your presence, LORD” (Ps. 89:15). You don’t just make joyful sound, you must learn how make it. You learn it in the presence of God, in intimate fellowship with the Holy Spirit. In Psalm 16:11, we learn a significant truth: “You make known to me the path of life; you will fill me with joy in your presence, with eternal pleasures at your right hand.” The revelation to the path of life, is to be filled with joy in His presence. It is in His presence we discover His eminence. When we make a joyful noise, we do it with songs and psalms in our hearts (Eph. 5:19, Psa. 105:2), with our voices (Psa. 149:1), with instruments (Psa. 150:4) everywhere, lifting up holy hands (1 Tim. 2:8).

II. Rejoice in God’s Benevolence

We rejoice in His benevolence, in what He has done for us. Is there anyone God has not done anything good in the past for? No! Except those who will say God has not done enough for them. The Bible says, He daily loads with benefits (Ps.68:19). He bears our burdens, day by day. For each day, Hear bears us in His arms. God's benefits are not few nor light, they are loads; neither are they intermittent, but they come daily; nor are they confined to one or two favourites, for all flesh, whether they acknowledge it or not, can say, “he loads us with benefits.” This was what Israel could not acknowledge at Rephidim (Exo. 17:1-7) what God had done for them. First, God heard their cry. He sent them a leader and a deliverer in Moses. He shielded their land of Goshen from the plagues (Exo.9:26) He sent upon Egypt. He gave them favour before the mourning Egyptians who paid them their 430 years wages. The pursuing Pharaoh and his chariots could not catch up with Israel. God opened up the Red Sea for their escape and better still all their stubborn pursuers perished in the Red Sea. For 30 days they were not hungry (Exo. 16:1). They murmured, they argued, they complained, they tested Him, they tried His patience. We must learn to take count of what God has done for us, so that our hearts won’t become callous with ingratitude. If we can’t recount anything, we can at least recall that He saved us from sin and death. And that is more than enough.

III. Rejoice in God’s Providence

We rejoice in God’s providence. The providence of God may be defined as His guardianship and care for His creatures and creation. God not only looks ahead and attempts to make provision for his goals, but infallibly accomplishes what he sets out to do. The Bible says, He works out everything in conformity with the purpose of his will (Eph.1:11). Whatever challenges or confrontations you might be going through now should not debar you from rejoicing in God, because we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose (Rom. 8:28). The present troubles are not even comparable to where the Lord is taking you (Rom. 8:18). His thoughts and plans and purposes for you are full of pleasantries. In fact, presently “He watches over” (Psa. 95:7b) and cares for you (1 Pet. 5:7). Sing praises to this great God. “Though thy beginning was small, yet thy latter end should greatly increase” (Job 8:7).]

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. How would you describe the attitude of the Israelites who are quick to forget God’s mercies and miracles on their behalf, and go into a murmuring frenzy at the slightest disappointment?

2. What does mean to say, “we make a joyful noise in spite of, not because of”?

3. Where’s the balance between not denying our feelings and yet not living by feelings, but by faith?

Lesson Summary

1. The call to make a joyful noise of praises and worship unto the Lord is a lifestyle of the believer.

2. We experience the fullness of joy in God’s presence, we therefore must cultivate an intimacy that keeps us connected with the Holy Spirit.

3. We can pray the same prayer as Paul, “Now may the God of hope fill you with all joy and peace in believing, that you may abound in hope by the power of the Holy Spirit” (Rom. 15:13).
