2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 6

The Baptism with the Holy Spirit

Lesson Starts

Text : Luke 3:16; Acts 1:8; Acts 2:1-7

Memory Verse: “John answered them all, "I baptize you with water. But one more powerful than I will come, the thongs of whose sandals I am not worthy to untie. He will baptize you with the Holy Spirit and with fire. 17 His winnowing fork is in his hand to clear his threshing floor and to gather the wheat into his barn, but he will burn up the chaff with unquenchable fire." - Luke 3:16-17

Bottom Line : Jesus is the One who baptizes people with the Holy Spirit.

Lesson Objective : In this lesson teens will learn the truth about how Jesus is connected to the baptism of the Holy Spirit.

Lesson Introduction

The Baptism of the Holy Spirit is an experience that every believer must long for. It is a wonderful experience to receive the Holy Spirit. The Holy Spirit comes into the life of an individual to empower the person for great exploits including witnessing for Jesus Christ.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

John the Baptist’s ministry was making waves, as he went about preaching repentance to them. The people thronged to him to be baptized at the Jordan, wondering if he was the Messiah. John would not steal the identity of the Christ. He took the opportunity to announce to the people that the Messiah was coming and that he was going to baptize them with the Holy Spirit. John’s audience may not have understood what he was saying, until the day of Pentecost, when the promise of the baptism of the Holy Spirit came to pass while the disciples were gathered together in the Upper Room. It was a dramatic experience as the 120 in the room were all filled with the Holy Spirit and began to speak in tongues they didn’t speak before.

Lesson Commentary

I. Baptism By John

Prior to the coming of John the Baptist, the Jews were familiar with various practices of ceremonial baths and washings, prescribed in the Mosaic Law to remove defilement and infection and restore the normal clean state (Lev 11-15). Proselytes (non-Jews, otherwise called gentiles, who converted to the Jewish religion), were required to be baptized as a ceremonial cleansing from defilement in addition to circumcision. Baptism, however, became popular with John’s public ministry, hence the appellation, ‘John the Baptist’. As a forerunner of the Messiah, John baptized Jews repentant of their sins, in response to his call to repentance. His baptism was symbolic with his message, which heralded the coming of the Christ and invariably the advent of the Kingdom of God. The baptism of the repentant symbolized the desire for forgiveness of sin, renouncing past life, and a desire to be included in the coming messianic kingdom. That was why John emphasized that the Jews who came to be baptized should quit deluding themselves with their Abrahamic heritage and “produce fruit in keeping with repentance” (Luke 3:8) rather than merely fulfilling a ceremonial cleansing rite. Significantly, Jesus who had no need for repentance also came to John to be baptized. John at first was reluctant, as he knew who Jesus was and felt unworthy to baptize him, but Jesus insisted (Matt. 3:14-15). By submitting to John’s baptism, Jesus demonstrated an act of obedience in doing the needful. He also validated the ministry and message of John, who proclaimed the imminence of the Kingdom of heaven and its Messiah. Jesus also stepped forward to be baptized not as an individual but as a representative of all who will repent and receive the Kingdom of God through Christ. Jesus’ stooping to be baptized by John indicted the arrogant and self-righteous Pharisees who refused to submit to the baptism of John (Luke 7:29-30). Finally, the baptism was an ample opportunity for the divine inauguration of the ministry of Jesus (Luke 3:21-22).

II. Baptism By Jesus

John baptized `with water, but John the Baptist himself affirmed that Jesus was the one to baptize with the “Holy Spirit and with fire”. Just before his ascension, Jesus also told the disciples, “For John baptized with water, but in a few days you will be baptized with the Holy Spirit” (Acts 1:5). While the coming of Jesus did not obliterate baptism with water, (as it became a Christian rite to symbolize repentance and acceptance of Christ), the baptism of the Holy Spirit refers to the endowment of the Christian with the power of the Holy Spirit, which is expressed in the regenerative work of the Holy Spirit at new birth and a further empowerment for living and service in God’s Kingdom. The convicting, converting, purifying, enlightening and energizing work of the Holy Spirit in the life of the believer is the literal composition of the metaphor to “baptize with Holy Spirit and with fire” as used by John in the text. How then is Jesus connected to all this? Certainly, the baptism of the Holy Spirit is Jesus’ prerogative, In John 7:37 we read: “Now on the last day of the feast, the great day, Jesus stood up and proclaimed, 'If anyone thirst, let him come to me and drink. He who believes in me, as the scripture has said, "Out of his heart shall flow rivers of living water."” And then in v. 39: “Now this he said about the Spirit, which those who believed in him were to receive; for as yet the Spirit had not been given, because Jesus was not yet glorified.” In John 15:26 Jesus spoke of the Counselor “whom I shall send to you from the Father… he will bear witness to me.” In John 16:13 Jesus declares that the Spirit when He comes will guide the disciples into all truth, and He will show them things to come.

Discussion Questions/Activities :

1. If the baptism of John was for sinners who sought repentance, why did Jesus submit himself to John’s baptism, since he had no sin?

2. In what way(s) was John’s baptism preparatory to the ministry of Christ?

3. Read Acts 2:39, how would you respond to those who claim the baptism of Holy Spirit ceased after the Pentecost experience with apostles?

Lesson Summary

We receive the Holy Spirit the moment we receive Jesus Christ as Lord and Savior (1 Cor. 12:13). In Ephesians 1:13-14, we learn that the Holy Spirit is the seal of salvation for all believers. This means it is the Holy Spirit who guarantees the believer’s eternal destiny. This shows how significant the presence and role of the Holy Spirit is in the life of the Christian. John’s message and baptism stirred up the people’s hope for the coming messianic kingdom, but the baptism of the Holy Spirit qualifies and empowers believers for the life of the Kingdom. The key to being baptized in the Holy Spirit is by receiving Christ. Like the religious Jews in John’s day who were familiar with all the ceremonial rites of the Mosaic Law and claimed the right to the heritage of Father Abraham, without showing any fruit of repentance, you may also be beclouded into thinking you have been in church for a long time. Perhaps you were even born into a Christian family, and your parents are church leaders, and you may have learnt all the lingoes of church, and practiced how to mutter some glossolalia. Quit playing church and get real. Take a reality check and establish where you stand with Christ, so you can begin to enjoy life with the Spirit.
