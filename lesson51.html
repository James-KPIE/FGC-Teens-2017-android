Lesson 51

Powerful Prayer

Lesson Starts

Text: James 5

Memory Verse: “Therefore confess your sins to each other and pray for each other so that you may be healed. The prayer of a righteous man is powerful and effective.” - James 5:16

Bottom Line: Prayer that does not doubt wins the deal.

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson, teens will

understand that God is ready to answer every prayer that is prayed with right motives and is in the will of God.

Lesson Introduction

Life is full of ups and downs. There is no person that is free from challenges/needs in life. Consider the needs you have in your personal life, in your family, in your career or education, with respect to your friends in school, work or neighbourhood. The problems, heartaches, difficulties – how great and numerous they are! These are things we experience and they drive us to God in prayer. Can you imagine what makes possible the wonderful power after praying? “The earnest prayer of the righteous man makes tremendous power available – dynamic in its working.” God is available after praying. Consider what can be accomplished after we have prayed. The possibilities are limitless!

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The writer identified himself only as ‘James, a servant of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ” (1:1). We have great men of the Bible called James especially in the New Testament but this very James was believed to be the leader of the Church in Jerusalem (Acts 15:13) and Paul referred to him as “James the Lord’s brother” and in numbering him among the apostles” (Gal. 1:19). The purpose of this book was to emphasise the importance of putting our faith into work which is apt and timely. There is need for us to demonstrate our salvation by faith in holy living and make the world know that faith in transforming force of this faith requires it translated into practical realities. Similarly, this author gave a general counsel concerning matters affecting the spiritual life of the Church.

Lesson Outline

I. Prayer and Praise (James 5:13)

II. Prayer and Faith for Healing (James 5:14-18 )

Lesson Commentary

I. Prayer and Praise

The earlier discussion is a revelation of the fact that sometimes problems and challenging situations come our way, but we should exercise godly/positive behaviour in the face of affliction (James 5:7-12). With this background, in trial, the Christian’s highest duty and his greatest privilege is communion with God. This is the picture James is trying to paint. Is there any among you afflicted? He should pray (v.13a). Someone says “prayer is the bridge between panic and peace.” The line to heaven is always open. We are always to pray! Not only are we to pray, but we are to sing as well. We should remember God in life’s happy moment – He says, ‘Is any merry? Let him sing psalms’ (v.13b). Praise should fill our lips when life is joyful and there should be praise even under pressure when we remember the goodness of God (Eph. 5:18-20).

II. Prayer and Faith for Healing

Affliction comes in different forms and one of it is illness or sickness. Prayer and faith for healing are seen in three dimensions in the above verses:

The Privilege of Divine Healing (5:14-15a): We are enjoined to pray in times of illness. This serves dual purposes – it is our duty and our privilege in Christ. “Healing is children’s meat.” In the time of illness, elders are given the assignment of praying and anointing the sick, which must be done in faith believing the Lord to raise the sick up. It is noteworthy here that it is not the anointing oil that will work out the healing but the Lord through the prayer of faith offered for the sick by the elders. The prayer of faith has the promise of an answer.

Healing and Forgiveness (5:15b-16a): However, is sin the cause of all illnesses and sicknesses? (John 9:1-2). Sin can cause some specific sickness (John 5:14). Here is a promise of healing for the sick and forgiveness for the repentant. The admonition, “confess your sins to one another, and pray for one another, so that you may be healed” (v.16a) is essential, is needful so that we might receive help for our trouble.

Effective Prayer (5:16b-18): The synonym for effectual are: capable, effective, efficacy, capability, efficacious etc. What is effective prayer? It is the prayer that comes from a righteous man (16b). A man whose heart is perfect before God! A man who is in right relationship with God and man. Do we have men whose prayer were effective? Yes! We have examples of such like Elijah – he was a man of like nature with ourselves (1 Kings 17:1, 18:1, 42-45). He was a man exactly like you and I, having the same opportunity and resources available from God that are available to us. Any true child of God must be ready to pray the prayer of faith that will bring healing. Elijah’s prayer for rain assures us that God answers prayer in the natural realm. Prayer not only changes us, but through it God also changes things.
