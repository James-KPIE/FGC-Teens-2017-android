2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 52

Give Glory to God

Lesson Starts

Memory Verse: “Suddenly a great company of the heavenly host appeared with the angel, praising God and saying. Glory to God in the highest, and on earth peace to men on whom his favor rests." Luke 2:13-14

Bottom Line: The birth of Jesus brought glory to God

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson, teens will learn the need to participate in bringing glory to God as they spread the Christmas message.

Lesson Introduction

Christmas is the celebration of the birth of Jesus Christ world-wide. Our celebration should however not just be merry making as is customary with very many, instead it should be a season to see beyond little Jesus in a manger in Bethlehem to the sacrificial Lamb of God on the cross at Calvary for the redemption and restoration of mankind. This Christmas should provoke in us rededication to our Lord and Saviour, as well as quicken us the more in the pursuit of God’s business of evangelism. Let our Christmas shoes take us beyond our Jerusalem to Judea, Samaria and to the uttermost part of the world. He is the best Christmas gift that man can give to a fellow man. Let’s give glory to our God as we celebrate Christmas.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

According to studies conducted by Prof. Fred A. Grissom, a professor of Religious Studies at North Carolina Wesley College, North Carolina, USA, the celebration of the birth of Christ (Christmas) did not exist until the middle ages. Its earliest observance is traceable to just before the fourth Century when Churches in the East (.i.e. Egypt, Asia Minor and Antioch) observed Epiphany (the manifestation of God to the world) where they celebrated Christ’s baptism, His birth and the visit of the angels. Early in the 4th Century however, Christians in Rome began to celebrate the birth of Christ. The practice spread widely and rapidly, such that by the end of the 4th Century, most parts of the Christian world began to observe the new festival. No clear evidence remains about the exact date of the birth of Christ. The December 25 date was chosen for both practical and theological reasons: until Christianity became the religion of the Roman Empire, the people in Rome celebrated the pagan feast of the ‘unconquerable sun’ on the 25th of December, to mark the beginning of the return of the sun. When Christianity became the religion of the empire, the Church replaced the festival of the sun with the festival of the Son – the light of the world. This is the popular origin of Christmas.

Lesson Commentary

I. The Birth of Jesus Christ

As foretold by the Prophet Micah (5:2), Jesus Christ was born in the city of Bethlehem. This came to be as a result of Caesar Augustus decree requesting that everyone in the then known world (everyone in the Roman Empire) be registered, possibly for the purpose of taxes. Joseph took his pregnant wife, Mary and they journeyed from Nazareth in Galilee where they live to Bethlehem in Judea to be counted. While in Bethlehem, Mary gave birth to Jesus in a stable and laid him in a manger, as there was no room for them to occupy in the inn. The birth of the Saviour of the world, the greatest event in all human history, occurred in the most humble of all places. Jesus is the King of kings, but He was neither born nor did he live like a king in his life time. God’s children are kings and priests (Rev. 1:6) but in this life, we must be as Jesus was, humble and simple.

II. The Herald of His Birth

After the birth of Jesus, an angel came down from heaven to gladly inform the shepherds in the open field keeping watch over their flock by night, of this historic event. The angel and a multitude of heavenly host praised God, saying “glory to God in the highest, and on earth, peace, goodwill towards man.” The shepherds hurried to see baby Jesus, and having done so, went about glorifying and praising God and spreading the good news of the birth of the Saviour. Jesus charges us to do same – spread the good news of the Saviour (Mark 16:15).

Discussion Questions / Activities

1. How did Christmas come to be?

2. From the brief history in the advent of Christmas, do you think God is interested in it?

3. Jesus is the King of kings, yet in his life time, right from birth, he lived a simple and humble life. What lesson can we learn from His attitude?

Lesson Summary

1. Jesus Christ was born in a manger. Although He is God, He made himself nothing, taking the very nature of a servant (Philippians 2:6-7). This is He did to be able to reach out and save the world. Our attitude should be the same as that of Jesus Christ. We must come off our high horses and humble ourselves so we can reach out to save others for Christ.

2. In the story of the birth, we notice that God chose very simple people to witness and spread the event of Jesus’ birth. This is instructive as God is no respecter of persons, in appointing people to carry out His purpose. You should never think that you are too small or insignificant to God’s plan. If He can use those lowly shepherds, He can use you.

3. The shepherds spread the word concerning what had been told them about Jesus. In the same manner, we should go out there and keep spreading good news about the Jesus event, we have experienced in our lives. This way we give God the glory.

Assignment

Christ was born into this world to serve mankind and He did. Give Him His due glory. Take Him this week to a soul that needs to receive the good news about Christ.
