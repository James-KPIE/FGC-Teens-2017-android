2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 50

Jesus’ Intercession for Us

Lesson Starts

Text: Hebrews 4:14–5:10

Memory Verse: “My prayer is not that you take them out of the world but that you protect them from the evil one.” - John 17:15

Bottom Line: Jesus is praying for us always

Lesson Objective: This lesson will assure the teens that Jesus is praying and interceding for them.

Lesson Introduction

A woman saw a little boy holding a sparrow with a broken wing. She said, “Son, would you like me to take this sparrow home and nurse it back to health? I promise to bring it back to you.” The little boy thought for a moment and then said “if you don’t mind, I’ll take care of it myself, because, you see, I understand this bird.” The woman didn’t understand what he meant until he stood. Then she saw that his left leg was in a cast. Because he was crippled, he understood the problem of the suffering bird.

Jesus is both human and divine. He came to earth and lived as a man. He was subjected to all the trials and testings’ of life, yet without sin. Therefore, as our High Priest, He is able to sympathise and empathise with us; He understands our feelings because He was tested and lived like us. He knows when we are lonely and misunderstood. He understands when we are discouraged, dejected and disappointed. He knows everything we are passing through. He is aware of all. As we go through this lesson, be assured that he will speak to you.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The Scripture in Matthew 6:9-13 is the picture of the prayer pattern the Lord taught His disciples. According to Kenneth Boa, there are eight forms of prayers from the above passages, which are: adoration, confession, renewal, petition, intercession, affirmation, thanksgiving and closing prayer. The focus of this lesson is an aspect of the Lord’s Prayer which is “intercession.”

Lesson Outline

I. Christ Our High Priest (Heb. 4:14-15, 15:1)

II. Christ Our Helper (Heb. 4:146)

Lesson Commentary

I. Christ our High Priest

Following the earlier discourse from the beginning of this book, chapters 1-4:13, the crux of the matter is Christ, as we see Christ’s glorious work and His exaltation, the pre-eminence of Christ’s message, pre-eminence of His glory and finally entering into rest by faith in Christ Jesus. Having charged us to pay more attention to God’s Word that we believe, the writer shifts to a dire need of “High Priest” – Jesus – the Son of God.

Since we believe that Christ is both divine and human, and by our confession of trust in Him, then His understanding should spur in us boldness and courage to approach Him – a direct access without any inhibition or constraint. Why? Because:

He understands our frailty - our weakness, feebleness.

He knows by experience what it means to be tempted.

Though, His kind of temptation may not be particularly like ours, but generally, He experienced temptation in the three basic areas of human predisposition: body, soul and spirit (physically, morally and spiritually).

What kind of infirmity/weakness/temptation/habits are we plagued with? How deep are we in such situation? How long has the situation been harassing and embarrassing us? Is there a way out? Like Paul said: “who can deliver me from…

Jesus, our high priest is the way out – of sickness, habit etc. He was tempted as we are, so He can sympathise with us. He was perfectly victorious. He faced temptation and did not sin.

II. Christ our Helper

To help means—to do part and to assist another to remedy etc. Help is an act of bearing another’s burden or support. There is a common saying that “heaven helps those who help themselves.” This is an untrue statement as regard spiritual walk of believers – “heaven helps those who cannot help themselves.” Christ is not only our intercessor, not only our mediator, He is also our helper who is willing and ready to help if only we can approach the throne of grace boldly in prayer. He is never asleep and there is no danger that you will be forgotten. Elijah ridiculed the prophets of baal by sacrificially suggesting their god was asleep when they needed him (1 Kings 18:27). But our God never sleeps nor slumbers. He is willing and ready to help you (Psa. 46:1, 121:1, Isa. 41:10). Which area of life do you need God’s help? Do you need help?

- To overcome sin

- To gain your freedom from harmful practices

- In your academic/career pursuit

- For healing in your body

- In joblessness

Lift your eyes above and beyond to the Lord who made heaven and earth.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. Who and who are Christ interceding for? (Jn. 17:9-17).

2. How is Christ praying for us? (Rom. 8:34, Heb. 7:25). Explain.

3. If Christ is interceding for us, why must we pray ceaselessly again? (1 Thess. 5:17).

Lesson Summary

Jesus is praying for you! Jesus is praying for me!! Jesus is praying for all those who have confessed Him their Lord and personal Saviour (Jn. 17:20). In His earthly ministry, He prayed for His disciple and others. And in Heaven from His right hand side of the Father, He is seated interceding for all those who are cleansed in His blood.
