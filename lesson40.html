2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 40

A Plea to Return to God (Independence Day)

Lesson Starts

Text: Malachi 3:1-12

Memory Verse: “Ever since the time of your forefathers you have turned away from my decrees and have not kept them. Return to me, and I will return to you," says the Lord Almighty. "But you ask, 'How are we to return?' "Will a man rob God? Yet you rob me. "But you ask, 'How do we rob you?' "In tithes and offerings.” - Malachi 3:7-8

Bottom Line: We must always be ready to right the wrongs when God searches us out.

Lesson Objective: This lesson will give opportunity to the teens to make things right with God.

Lesson Introduction

Proverbs 14:34 says: “Righteousness exalteth a nation, but sin is a reproach to any people.” The Bible tells us that our God and father is ever merciful. His mercy endures forever (Psa. 103:17; 106:1; 107:1; 117:2). He loves us and would not let the Devil destroy us and our eternal destiny. So, daily he beckons to us as a people and as a nation to come to Him and be saved from sin and death (Jn. 3:16; Rev. 3:19-20). He says to us to return to Him and He will return to us (Mal. 3:7; 1 Jn. 1:9). It is that simple. As Nigeria celebrates 57 years of political independence, the people must follow the path of righteousness and be freed from the effect of economic, social, and spiritual sins – the economic strangulation and cumulative hardship in the land is very enormous.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

Prophet Malachi is believed to have proclaimed God’s message to the post exilic Jews between 430-420 BC. This was the season when the people were again experiencing adversity and spiritual decline. They had become cynical, doubting God’s love and promises. They questioned His justice and disbelieved that there was any profit in obeying His commands. As their faith dimmed, they became mechanical and insensitive in their worship observance, indifferent to the law’s requirements and guilty of all kinds of sins against the covenant. Malachi confronted both the priests and the people with the following prophetic summons:

1. To repent of their sins and religious hypocrisy before God.

2. To remove the obstacles of disobedience that were blocking the flow of God’s favour and blessing, and

3. To return to the Lord and His covenant with sincere and obedient hearts.

Lesson Outline

I. Malachi Foretells God’s Visitation

II. Malachi Calls The People to Repentance

Lesson Commentary

I. Malachi Foretells God’s Visitation

In verses 1-5 of our text for this lesson, Prophet Malachi foretells of the visitation of God on the people. His visitation is a double-edge of both salvation and judgment. This double impact act would be fulfilled by the Messiah. The Messiah will come as a refiner and purifier of silver. He would purge and purify the priests as gold and silver, so that they would be sensitive in their worship observance and offer unto the Lord an offering in righteousness. This is the offering acceptable to God – in the past and at all times (Psa. 4:5; Rom. 6:13; 1 Pet. 2:5). Remember we are the present day priests of the Lord (1 Pet. 2:9). This message from Malachi spoke concerning Christ’s first and second coming. Christ has come offering salvation to all and sanctifying to all who receive Him (as individuals and nations). When He comes again, it would be to judge the world—both individuals and nations. He will purge out all the wicked in the land. Only the righteous will remain (cf. Isa. 1:25, 26; Ezek. 22:17-22).

II. Malachi Calls The People to Repentance

In Malachi’s message, God reminds and rebukes the people that they had long strayed from His ordinances (laws) and had thus refused to keep them right from the days of their fathers. They had refused to take offering and tithes to God, no wonder they suffered lack, loss and agonizing deprivation. Then out of His great love for His people, He asks them to correct their ways and return to Him so He could restore them and bless them. His words in Malachi 3:7b, 12: “Return unto me, and I will return unto you, saith the Lord of hosts.” “And all nations shall call you blessed: for ye shall be a delightsome land, saith the Lord of hosts.” What God says to Israel, He says to His Church and to us teenagers. Let us boldly right all wrongs as God searches us out through His Spirit in us. That is our only means to restoration and lasting blessings. Then all nations shall call us blessed and we shall be a delightsome people to God’s glory.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. How significant are we as teenagers to the nation?

2. In what ways are we to prepare the way for the coming of the Lord?

3. What other lessons are we to learn from today’s lesson?

Lesson Summary

1. Teenagers as succeeding generations of every nation, must respond swiftly to the plea to return to God, especially in the face the growing decadence in the world.

2. Christian teenagers must see themselves messengers of God, sent to prepare the world for the coming of Christ, in a lifestyle of obedience to God, in evangelizing and in prayers for the nation and non-Christians.

3. Our memory verse is Malachi 3:7 “Even from the day of your fathers ye are gone away from mine ordinances, and have not kept them. Return unto me, and I will return unto you, saith the Lord of hosts.”

Assignment

Assignment

Go and read Malachi 3:1-18 to yourself, and examine your relationship with God in the light of this chapter of scripture. Write out what is needful for you to do. Prayerfully begin with the first one till you get to the last one.
