2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 36

A Redeemer in Zion

Lesson Starts

Text: Isaiah 59:1-21

Memory Verse: "The Redeemer will come to Zion, to those in Jacob who repent of their sins," declares the Lord.” - Isaiah 59:20

Bottom Line: There will always be deliverance for God’s people.

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson, the teens will be able to trust God more than ever before.

Lesson Introduction

In Hebrew, a redeemer is the nearest kinsman or what is now known as nearest of kin. The redeemer or kinsman-redeemer is saddled with three rights, which also could be referred to as roles. First, to buy back the forfeited inheritance for an Israelite who, through poverty, had sold his land; as Boaz did for Ruth (Ruth 4:3-5); or to hold land in possession for an impoverished kinsman until the year of jubilee, when it should be returned to the original owner (Lev. 25:10,13-16,24-28). Second, the kinsman-redeemer ransoms his kinsman from slavery to the foreigner (Lev. 25:47-49) and finally, the kinsman-redeemer reserves the right to avenge the death of his slain kinsman as a matter of honor.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

At this point in the history of Judah the people were in need of a Redeemer. They looked up to God to redeem them from their predicament, but there seemed to be no help in sight. They were in a dilemma—they were perplexed as to why God was not answering their prayers. They wondered why God would not come to rescue them from the assault of their enemies – the Assyrians.

Lesson Commentary

I. The Rebellion and Retribution of Zion

God’s responds to them through prophet Isaiah. He tells them that the reason He has not answered their prayers was not due to any lack of ability on His part. On the contrary, it was because of their sins: Their hands were soiled with blood, and their fingers with iniquity (v.3). They spoke lies and mutter injustice (v.3b). They were dishonest, trusting in empty and worthless words (v.4). They conceived trouble and concocted immorality (v.4). Their feet ran after evil and they rushed to shed innocent blood (v.7). Their thoughts were sinful (v.7b). As a result of their sins God left them to be invaded and oppressed by their enemies. Their iniquities “built barriers” between them and God, and their sins caused Him to “hide His face” from them so as not to listen to their pleadings.

II. The Redemption and Restoration of Zion

Hitherto, the people of Judah have had a checkered history of rebelling against God, facing retribution and then returning to God. No sooner had God granted them pardon, than they returned to their old ways. The circle then begins again. This time they really did it, so much that there was no one to intercede on their behalf. They couldn’t save themselves. And no one could stand in for them. God had to step in to do the Redeemer’s job Himself. This goes to show that salvation is of the Lord. Upon the people’s acknowledgement of their sins, (vv.12-13) God moves to deliver them, for even the lawful captive shall be delivered (Isa. 49:24-25). In the immediate, Yahweh delivers his people from the bondage of their captors, just like it is expected of a kinsman-redeemer. However, in the ultimate, he promises them a Redeemer, who will come to redeem those who turn away from their sins. This was referring to the Messiah, Jesus Christ, who would save them from their sins and the consequences thereof.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. In what ways can sin be a barrier between us and God?

2. Of what importance is repentance to God’s redemptive act?

3. In what ways does Christ fulfil the roles of the kinsman-redeemer in our lives?

Lesson Summary

Lesson Summary

This promise of a Redeemer is not to Israel alone. It is a promise to all people who will respond to the call of repentance (v.19). This promise is two-fold; first that the Messiah will come to be our Redeemer and second, that the Spirit of God will come to be our sanctifier and the seal of our eternal destiny (v.21). God’s promised salvation is for all who believe His words and with whom His Spirit has entered. David Guzik puts it this way, “Jesus Christ is our near kinsman… He is the one who buys us out of [the] slavery [of sin]. He is the one who avenges wrongs done to us. He protects our inheritance…” What more? His Holy Spirit is given to us as a guarantor of our inheritance in Christ. And sometime in the future, the Redeemer will come (referring to the second coming of Jesus). He will come to receive all those who have asked God’s forgiveness and have been saved through the blood of Jesus. Then we would ultimately take possession of our inheritance.
