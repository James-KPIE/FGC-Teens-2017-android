2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 25

Honour your Father

Lesson Starts

Text: Exodus 20:12; Ephesians 6:1-3; Colossians 3:20

Memory Verse: “Children, obey your parents in everything, for this pleases the Lord.” - Colossians 3:20

Bottom Line: Dads are pretty special and deserve to be honoured.

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson, teens will respond positively to their father’s request for obedience and respect.

Lesson Introduction

As the Church celebrates Father’s Day today, let us go down memory lane to trace the origin of father’s day. Mrs. John B. Dodd of Washington D.C was the first to propose the idea of a father’s day in the year 1909, to honour her father, William Smart, a civil war veteran who raised her and five others on a rural farm as a single parent after the death of their mother. The first father’s day was observed on June 19, 1910 in Spokane, Washington as well as other cities across America. In 1924, President Calvin Coolidge supported the idea of a national father’s day and in 1966, President Lyndon Johnson signed a presidential proclamation declaring 3rd Sunday of June annually as father’s day. Beyond the commemoration of the day, we must acknowledge that honouring our fathers is not just a social conduct or value, but a very significant spiritual principle.

More importantly however, obeying and honouring parents is God’s principle to long and satisfying life (it may be well with you) and you may live long (Eph. 6:3; Ex. 20:12).

It is a spiritual demand. God commands that we obey and honour our parents so that it may be well with us and that we may live long (Eph. 6:1-2). So, when we obey and honour our fathers, mothers and spiritual parents, we are obeying and honouring God. Of course, the attached blessings are well-being and long life following automatically.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

Parental honour is the first of the laws in the last six of the Ten Commandments that define our duty to one another as human beings and to our relations. The commandment to honour our parents also falls into the second category of Jesus’ great commandment, “Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself” (Luke 10:27). In other words, parental honour must be motivated by love. Love for our neighbour is certainly not an option. Therefore, honouring our dads is a loving imperative that cannot be negotiated and should not be compromised. Jesus took up the Pharisees, who had eroded the principle of parental honour with their traditions, shirking their responsibilities to parents under the guise of their commitment to God (Matthew 15:3-6). It is not surprising then that Paul reemphasized the principle as a New Testament teaching with benefits (Eph.6:3).

Lesson Outline

II. God Rewards Honour to Father

Lesson Commentary

I. God Commands Honour to Father

There is no mix up about this matter at all. The Bible is quite clear about it. It is an imperative statement: “children, obey your parents in all things: for this is well pleasing unto the Lord” Col.3:20). Psychologist Sigmund Freud provided an excuse for a generation to blame all of their problems on their forbearers. This kind of thinking is what have given rise to the anger and disrespect in many children against their parents, especially dads. But we are of a different breed, we are children of God, with God’s Spirit dwelling in us. We will obey God’s command to honour our fathers. We cannot be given to the practice of bitterness, contempt, and negligence towards our parents, as some children do. We never outgrow God’s command to honour our parents. Both our actions and attitudes should reveal our real motive for honouring our parents—to bring honour to God. The Greek word for honour is timao which means “to prize; to fix a value upon; to revere.” Honouring parents requires that we preserve their dignity as much as possible. Assisting them with household matters, errands, and health issues should never be considered burdensome, but an opportunity to be glorify God in our duty towards them. Honouring our dads (especially difficult ones) may not always be simple, pleasurable, or even reasonable. However, the understanding that God is being honoured when we honour them should be a good motivation.

II. God Rewards Honour to Father

God’s automatic reward to a child who obeys and honours his father (and mother) is a long and a satisfying life. As a teenager, you can and should be a source of encouragement to your father. When you are disciplined, obedient, and prove responsible and reliable, at home, in school, in church and wherever, you make your father proud of you. This is a great honour done him and he strives in return to satisfy you to the best of his ability. A father would strive to meet the needs of his obedient, loyal and responsible children at a huge sacrifice. Imagine how much more God can do. When we obey our father (and mother) in all things, God is pleased. We also get a bonus of a long and satisfying life (Eph. 6:3; Ex. 20:12). Remember, God is honoured when we honour our dads and vice versa. Read 1 Samuel 2:30B and meditate.

Discussion Questions / Activity

1. In what ways can we, as teenagers, honour our father (in particular, and parents in general)?

2. What could be the causes of disrespect to fathers/parents among teenagers, and how can we deal with them?

3. Read Matthew 15:3-6, how does the passage resonate with certain of our practices, under the pretext of serving God, which subtly defies the principle of parental honour?

Lesson Summary

1. God demands that children, teenagers inclusive, honour their father/parents at all times. Waning societal values and faulty concepts and philosophies that encourage subtle disrespect for fathers do not change God’s imperative to honour our dads.

2. This is the secret to living a long and rewarding life.

3. Our memory verse emphatically elucidate on our topic of the day.

Anticipating the next lesson

Assignment

Think of any special and beautiful way to honour your father. Go ahead and do it and observe how he responds, for class testimony.
