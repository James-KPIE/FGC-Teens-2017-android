2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 26

Child-Like Faith

Lesson Starts

Text : Luke 18:15-17, Mark 10:16-31

Memory Verse : “I tell you the truth, anyone who will not receive the kingdom of God like a little child will never enter it." And he took the children in his arms, put his hands on them and blessed them.” - Mark 10:15-16

Bottom Line : Simplicity of heart like a child in faith is the key to enter the Kingdom of God in Heaven.

Lesson Objective: This lesson will stir up simple and practical faith in the teens.

Lesson Introduction

A toddler once saw his Grandma visibly upset and in a rage, walks up to the Grandma and asks, “Grandma, how are you?” You can trust this simple question of concern quenched the fury and rage and got everyone laughing including the Grandma. That is a child for you; very simple, innocent, humble, unpretentious and spontaneous. This is what the Lord expects from us to come to Him in childlike faith; trusting His goodness, care, guidance and provisions of a Father.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

Jesus taught two parables about prayer at the beginning of this chapter; the first parable on persistent prayer and the other humility in prayer. After these teachings, parents brought their children to Jesus “that he would touch them” (Luke 18:15). Jesus’ disciples rebuked the parents forbidding the children to come to Jesus. Jesus showed His displeasure (Mark 10:14) when the disciples did this. Therefore, He rebuked the disciples, informing them that the kingdom of God is made up of believers who approach Him and His kingdom with faith like a child. Jesus then “took them up in His arms, put His hands upon them and blessed them” (Mark 10:16). By doing so, Jesus reaffirmed that children are very important to Him, as well as believers with embodied faith in him like a child.

Lesson Outline

I. In The Eyes of Man (Mark 10:16, Luke 18:15).

II. In The Eyes of God (Mark 10: 14, Luke 18:16)

III. In The Kingdom of God (LUKE 18:17)

Lesson Commentary

I. In The Eyes of Man

How do we regard little children concerning the kingdom? They are just little children! They are not that important now! They have a whole lot of time! What do they know? How much can they understand? This attitude often shows in our reaction to these children in spiritual, social and family issue. Perhaps this was the situation of the disciples, they considered the children more of nuisance and unimportant therefore the effort at preventing these children access to Jesus. Jesus didn’t agree with that kind of thinking. He told the disciples, “ Let the little children come to Me, and do not forbid them; for of such is the kingdom of God .”

II. In The Eyes of God

A. God sees differently. He looks at the heart (1 Sam. 16:7, Acts 10:34-35). When Jesus saw the behaviour of His disciples, He was greatly displeased. He said to them, “Let the little children come to Me, and do not forbid them; for of such is the kingdom of God ” (Mark 10:14).

B. Christ has room for everyone who comes empty- handed and trusting in Him (Luke 18:16). He took the children in His arms, placed His hands on them and blessed them. Those who have received Christ are called the children of God (Rom. 8:14-17).

III. In The Kingdom of God

Christ emphasized the need to receive the Kingdom of God as a little child. He acknowledged the beautiful qualities in little children; transparent, spontaneous, insistent and receptive to what God has freely given of which a believer must have to receive the kingdom.

A. Children are highly dependent. Believers are to be dependent on the Lord. (Pro. 3:5-6)

B. Children are very transparent, sincere and unpretentious.

C. Children are creatures of faith, they can be very insistent and trust easily. Believers are to be people of faith, have faith in the Lord God.

D. Children are very open and receptive. They do not have inhibitions and are naturally inquisitive and want to learn. Believers are to be open to God’s leading.

E. Children are humble and innocent. Believers are to be humble before God (1 Pet. 5:5-6).

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. Identify ways we unintentionally restrict others access to Jesus Christ.

2. Discuss ways we can practically demonstrate faith like a child

3. What must people do to receive the kingdom of God? (vv.16-17)

Lesson Summary

Christ laid much emphasis on our becoming as little children in our faith to enter the kingdom of heaven (Matt. 18:1-4). True disciples are to approach Christ with childlike faith; believe and follow Him without reservation. May the Lord stir up in us simple and childlike faith that trusts in His goodness, care, guidance and provisions.
