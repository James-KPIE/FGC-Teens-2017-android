2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 19

Propagating Faith

Lesson Starts

Text : Luke 8:26-39

Memory Verse : “When those tending the pigs saw what had happened, they ran off and reported this in the town and countryside” - Luke 8:34

Bottom Line : No normal person can keep good news to himself or herself without sharing it.

Lesson Objective: This lesson will help the teens to begin to share the goodness of Jesus Christ in their lives to everyone around them.

Lesson Introduction

In these days of depressing news, gloom and uncertainty all around, good news will be very welcomed and heart lifting. “The light of the eye rejoices the heart, and a good report makes the bones healthy” (Pro. 15:30 NKJV). What is really good news to a believer? The gospel is good news. This news is that Jesus died and rose again to pay the penalty for our sins so that we might become children of God through faith in the resurrected Christ (Eph. 2:8). We are appointed and anointed to preach and proclaim this good tidings to the world (Isa. 61:1-3).

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

Jesus chose the twelve to become His disciples (Luke 6:12-16). In preparation for the great mission ahead of them, He sent them out because He wanted to make sure they understood their mission and the difficulties ahead. He allowed them to share in experiences like calming a storm, deliverance for a demoniac, etc. (Luke 6:17-9:6). Each of Jesus’ encounters taught His disciples about His ministry and what would be required of them as they went into the world as His representatives. In our text today, we are shown the purpose Jesus had in setting sail from Capernaum in Galilee. Jesus was willing to go to great lengths of crossing the land and the great sea, to save one tortured soul. The text highlights two very important points, that Jesus Christ is Lord and has power against unclean spirits, to cast them out, and to heal all manner of sickness and disease. The story of the demoniac is a great illustration of what satanic forces can do to a human personality under their control. Jesus came to destroy the work of the devil and give us life abundantly (1 John 3:8, John 10:10b). He has power over satanic forces; Satan and demons recognize the authority of Jesus and obey Him (Luke 8:29, 32).

Lesson Outline

I. The Prisoner (Luke 8:26,29)

II. The Proclaimer (LUKE 8: 26,29)

Lesson Commentary

I. The Prisoner

We are introduced to the story of this man of Gadarenes, sorely possessed and tormented by the devil.

A. The state of the man before his encounter with Jesus Christ was described thus (vv.26, 29);

1. Naked

2. Unrestrained

3. Uncontrollable

4. Living among the dead

The man had lost his identity to demons. He bore their name and was controlled by their power.

B. The good news is that Christ came to save the lost and set the captives free (Luke 19:10, John 3:17, 1 Tim. 1:15). Demons are subject to His power. They tremble at His presence. The devil’s power may be great but the Word of God is more powerful, for all power belongs to Him (Matt. 28:18).

1. When the demon-possessed man encountered Christ, Christ demonstrated His power against unclean spirits, to cast them out, and to heal all manner of sickness and all manner of disease (Mat. 10:1)

2. Jesus meets man at his greatest needs. The state of the man after his encounter with Jesus was described thus (LK. 8: 33, 35-36)

a. Delivered, no longer possessed and tormented

b. Seated peacefully at Jesus feet (a disciple), no longer unrestrained or uncontrollable

c. Clothed , no longer naked

d. In his right mind, no longer subject to wild and self-destructive behaviour

e. Healed holistically- mentally, emotionally, physically, socially and spiritually, no longer under the influence of the demonic spirit that made him very unstable mentally and emotionally while physically violent.

f. Saved completely (v.36), no longer unsaved and tormented

This is the Good News!!! “ For the Son of Man came to seek and to save the lost ” (Luke 19:10, Mat. 18:11)

II. The Proclaimer

A. What do we do with the Good news? Proclamation is what you do with the good news. Good news cannot be hidden or withheld. It is normal to tell or share it with people. It will make the headlines. There is no greater tool for proclaiming the power of God than the lips of the one who has experienced the power of God first hand.

Good news is to be:

1. Proclaimed

2. Preached

3. Published

4. Publicized (Isa. 52:7, 61:1-3, Rom. 10:15)

B. The Proclaimers are:

1. They that fed the swine (Luke 8: 34, 36). They went and told it in the city and in the country. Whether in good or bad faith, they proclaimed the good news (Phil. 1:15-17). They narrated how and by what means the man was delivered.

2. The man who was delivered (vv. 37-39). He reacted in faith and not fear, and wanted to go with Jesus, but he commissioned him to Go and Tell. “Return to thine own house, and shew how great things God hath done unto thee. And he went his way, and published throughout the whole city how great things Jesus had done unto him.

He went to

a. His own house, family, neighbours, community

b. Showed the great things God had done for him

c. Published it throughout the whole city.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. Discuss practical ways to share the good news with friends and peers.

2. Let us sing

“What the Lord has done for me I cannot tell it all 3x

He saved me and washed me in His Blood

So I can shout Hallelujah, I can shout Hallelujah

I can shout, Praise the Lord!!

3. Go and Tell the good news!!!

Lesson Summary

Our faith should be more than just coming to Church, fellowshipping and receiving blessing. It involves returning to the World and declaring our experience with God. Our greatest witness of the power of God is what He has done in our lives and our testimonies should be of ongoing and growing experience.
