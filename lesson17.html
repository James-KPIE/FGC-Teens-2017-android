2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 17

Struggling Faith

Lesson Starts

Text : Mark 14:26-31, 66-72

Memory Verse : “So, if you think you are standing firm, be careful that you don't fall!” - 1 Corinthians 10:12

Bottom Line : No power or might that is exhibited in the flesh can be sustained.

Lesson Objective: This lesson will help teens to fully rely on God in all things and everything.

Lesson Introduction

The Christian life seems to be a bit of a battle most of the time.

In an age when we are bombarded every day with music, magazines on the shelf, billboards on the road and movies all sending out the wrong messages, we are faced with pressures that takes their toll on our faith. We live in a culture where people’s priority is to keep up with the newest styles, go with the flow, work out, stay trim, have a boyfriend or girlfriend, and get into school, get a really good job, drive a cool car and be the happening guy. Quite a suffocation, and it could be so frustrating when you try to get a bit spiritual, you want to do the right thing, you want to rep Christ, you even but you seem to be making so little progress. Sometimes you are up, but most of the time you are very aware of your downs. Everything is a struggle: you struggle with reading your Bible, you struggle with praying, you struggle not to tell a lie, you struggle with lust, and you struggle with self-control. There’s no end to the list. If this is the way you feel, you need to know that you are not alone.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

Jesus had announced His impending death earlier to His disciples several times before this final warning. During this final warning and after instituting the Lord’s Supper, they sang a hymn and went out into Mount of Olives. As they were leaving, Jesus announced that all of them will desert him. Peter, so cocksure, rejected the idea that at least he could never turn his back on Jesus. Jesus responds to Peter specifically, telling him that he will deny Him three times before sunrise. Then Peter vehemently boasted that he was willing to die for Jesus. Peter had good intention but it seems that Peter never imagined that his commitment would be put to a test.

Lesson Outline

I. Causes of Struggling Faith

II. Consequences of Struggling Faith

III. Cure for Struggling Faith

Lesson Commentary

I. Causes of Struggling Faith

Struggling faith is a wavering or faltering faith (Heb. 10:23, Jam. 1:6), staggering faith (Rom. 4:20), stumbling faith (Rom. 9:31-32). What causes our faith to struggle or waver? Let us explore from the text given to us.

A. Self-confidence and pride. The problem of “I.” “But Peter said unto him, “Although all shall be offended, yet will not I” (Mark 14:29-31, Pro. 3:5).

B. Lack of watchfulness (v.34, 37, 40).

C. Problem of sin/flesh (v.38).

D. Fear and threats (vv.66-71).

E. Focus on circumstances rather than God vv.66-71).

F. Ignorance of the Word of God.

II. Consequences of Struggling Faith

What are the manifestations or symptoms of struggling Faith?

A. Denial of faith: Failure to stand up when it matters most, to stand up for Christ, or for righteousness.

B. Becoming unstable (James 1:6-8)

C. Misleading others (vv.66-71)

D. Making wrong and costly decisions (v.71)

E. Loss of peace and joy (v.72). Peter wept bitterly.

III. Cure for Struggling Faith

A. Being strong in the Lord (Eph. 6:10, Psa. 28:7-8)

B. Drawing strength from the Word (Psa. 119:28)

C. Waiting on the Lord (Isa. 40:29-31)

D. Understanding the sufficiency of God’s grace (2 Cor. 12:9-10)

E. Taking your stand and courageously choosing to obey (Acts 5:29, Dan. 3:15-18).

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. In what ways do we compromise our faith?

2. Would your faith hold up under the pressure of trial and temptation or even persecution?

3. What does it mean to stand up for Jesus?

Lesson Summary

Peter’s denial of Christ was heart rendering, “ I do not know this Man of whom you speak!” Both from his action and speech, he failed Jesus. He trusted in himself and flesh failed him. Our confidence must never rest in our ability but in the power of God to overcome life challenges.
