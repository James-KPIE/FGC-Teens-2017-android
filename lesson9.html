2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 9

The Holy Spirit and the Great Commission

Lesson Starts

Text : Joel 2:28-29; Zechariah 4:6; Matt.28:18-20; Acts 1:4-5

Memory Verse: “But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, 23 gentleness and self-control. Against such things there is no law.” - Acts 1:8

Bottom Line : The Holy Spirit was given to the disciples at Pentecost, so we also are given when we believe

Lesson Objective : In this lesson, the Teens will learn that the Holy Spirit is a gift from God and the import of this gift in the life of a believer in evangelizing.

Lesson Introduction

The presence of the Holy Spirit in the life of the believer is to enable the believer to live his life as a soul winner for Christ. He has been given the Holy Spirit so that he can receive power to be a witness for the Lord Jesus Christ. The preaching of the gospel is the primary assignment of every believer in Christ. We are to go out to all parts of the world to witness for the Lord Jesus Christ.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The promise that the Lord Jesus Christ made to the disciples before his ascension was to baptize them with the Holy Spirit. The power that the disciples will receive after the Holy Spirit has come upon them will enable them to be able to stand firm in preaching the gospel of the Lord Jesus Christ. The power will make them to become very bold and to withstand any form of opposition that comes their way. Whatever they are able to accomplish while preaching the gospel will be ascribed to be the work of the Holy Spirit. They will be able to baptize the converts in the name of the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit as well as teaching the converts to obey all that Jesus Christ has commanded.

Lesson Outline

I. Preaching The Gospel Before The Baptism of The Holy Spirit

II. Preaching The Gospel After The Baptism of The Holy Spirit

III. Reaching out to Jerusalem and Other Places

Lesson Commentary

I. Preaching The Gospel Before The Baptism of The Holy Spirit

Before the disciples were baptized of the Holy Spirit, they were able to go out under the directive of the Lord Jesus Christ to various cities and villages to preach the gospel. However, their preaching was limited in scope as well as the attested signs and wonders that followed there ministration. The results of conversion that followed their preaching were limited. They encountered cases of needs for healings and deliverances that they could not do anything about (Matt. 17:16, John 9:2, Mark 3:14, Luke 9:2).

II. Preaching The Gospel After The Baptism of The Holy Spirit

After the disciples were baptized of the Holy Spirit, their ministrations and the demonstration of the power of the Holy Spirit changed drastically. They were able to preach with boldness, they were no longer afraid of persecutions from the priests, Romans and fellow Jews. Peter was able to preach with such boldness that was not there before the baptism of the Holy Spirit. The result of his message brought great conversion as many people gave their lives to Christ and were baptized in water (Acts 2:14,37-41).

III. Reaching out to Jerusalem and Other Places

The gospel is to be preached first in Jerusalem, then Judea, Samaria and unto the uttermost part of the world. This simply means that we are to begin the preaching of the gospel from our immediate environment and our immediate neighborhood before we venture out to places farther from where we are located. No place must be too far for us to reach out to with the gospel (Rom. 15:31, Acts 8:5, Act 18:1, Acts 19:21).

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. How can one reach his immediate family with the gospel?

2. How can the people of our neighborhood be reached?

3. How can we extend our outreach of the preaching of the gospel to places and people far from us?

Lesson Summary

  1. With the coming of the Holy Spirit, preaching the Gospel just got easy and more effective.
  1. We need to advantage of the influence and dynamism of the Holy Spirit to preach the Gospel.
  1. There are various means today readily made available to us to reach out to the people around us on daily basis with the gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ. The available means are quite endless, ranging from personal witnessing which involves going out to meet people in their homes, workplace, etc., to calling through phone, using social media platform, using tracts, using the multimedia channels.
  1. The Holy Spirit can use us in facilitating any of these channels to reach out to lost souls.

Anticipating the next lesson

The presence of the Holy Spirit in today's church is evident in various ways. The influence of the Holy Spirit upon the church has been interpreted in various ways ranging from His dovelike appearance to the winds, to the oil and so on.
