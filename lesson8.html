2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 8

The Gifts of the Holy Spirit

Lesson Starts

Text: 1 Corinthians 12:4-11

Memory Verse: “There are different kinds of gifts, but the same Spirit.” – 1 Corinthians 12:4

Bottom Line: Spiritual gifts are special abilities given by the Holy Spirit to help Christians grow stronger in their relationship with the Lord.

Lesson Objective: In this lesson Teens will discover the different kinds of spiritual gifts that are given by God when a person is filled with the Holy Spirit

Lesson Introduction

We are all born with unique abilities in life. This uniqueness is what differentiates from one another. Similarly, as Christians, we are uniquely and differently endowed with the gifts of the Holy Spirit. However, the diversity of our endowment is meant to be complementary and not competitive. It is supposed build unity among us, as believers and to be divide us.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

In 1 Corinthians 12, Apostle Paul revealed the gifts of the Holy Spirit that has been released upon the church for the purpose of fulfilling the work of Christ on earth. Paul observed that there can be diversities of gifts, differences in administrations and diversities of operations but all these are controlled by the same Holy Spirit, the same Lord and the same God. In our text Paul highlights nine gifts. The nine are further categorized under three triads, namely: The Revelational Gifts, The Power Gifts and The Vocal Gifts.

Lesson Commentary

I. The Revelational Gifts

The revelational gifts are special abilities given to the believer to exercise supernatural knowledge, they are Wisdom, Knowledge and Discernment of spirits.

A. The word of wisdom comes into operation at any crucial moment when there is a word to be released to resolve any unclear issue facing the church (1Corinthians 12:8, 2Chronicles 1:11). King Solomon was blessed with the gift of the word of wisdom and in many instances he used the gift to resolve disputes that were brought to him (1 Kings 3:24-28, 4:29).

B. The word of knowledge is the ability to discover, analyze, and systematize truth for the benefit of others. With this gift, one speaks with understanding and penetration.

C. The discernment of spirits (note that it is not the spirit of discernment as popularly but wrongly designated) is the ability to discern the type of spirit in operation in any gathering or circumstance is a special gift. There are so many spirits in the world that are competing to get the attention of the church. When the identity of the spirit in operation is revealed, it puts to rest any uncertainty about which spirit is in operation.

II. The Power Gifts

The power gifts are abilities to exercise and accomplish supernatural acts. They include: Faith, Healings, and Miracles.

A. Faith is the ability to demonstrate strong faith in the midst of strong uncertainty. . This type of faith is different from the one available to every believer. It is a special ability to believe God for the most impossible thing.

B. The gifts of healings is the ability to be able to demonstrate healings in various dimensions upon the church. There are different kinds of sickness and diseases and there are different kinds of healings (1 Cor. 12:9; Acts 3:6-8; 6:5).

C. The working of miracles confer on the individual the ability to accomplish extraordinary or supernatural acts that will lead to the winning of more souls to the Lord Jesus Christ

III. The Vocal Gifts

The vocal gifts are gifts of supernatural utterance. The Holy enables the believer to speak the mind of God on issues pertinent to the body of Christ. They are Prophesy, Tongues, and Interpretation.

  1. The gift of prophesy is the ability to receive and proclaim a message from God. This could involve the foretelling of future events, though its primary purpose as seen in 1 Corinthians 14:3 is forth telling: one who prophesies speaks to people for their strengthening, encouragement, and consolation.
  1. Divers kinds of tongues is the ability to receive and impart a spiritual message in a language the recipient never learned. For other members of the body to be edified, this message must be interpreted either by the recipient (1 Cor. 14:13) or by another person with the gift of interpretation (1 Cor. 14:26-28).
  1. Interpretation of tongues (1 Cor. 12:10, 30; 14:5, 13, 26-28)--The ability to translate into the understandable language a message publicly uttered in a tongue. This gift may be combined with the gift of tongues (1 Cor. 14:13), or it can operate separately (1 Cor. 14:26-28).

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. Can any believer possess all these gifts?

2. Is there any gift that is superior to others?

3. How do one discover and develop spiritual gift? (This can be used as take home assignment)

Lesson Summary

  1. Every believer has the opportunity of receiving one or more of the gifts of Holy Spirit, but not all. The believer may desire and request for a gift, but it is prerogative of the Holy Spirit to give what to who.
  1. The Holy Spirit enables the manifestation of each gifts in the life of the believer for the benefit of the church.
  1. The gifts are not for personal gratification and shouldn’t lead to pride or conceit in recipient.
  1. No one gift is higher than the other and they are not mutually exclusive, the gifts must work together to build the church of Christ.
  1. It is important that every Christian discover and develop the spiritual gifts that have been bestowed on him or her by God (2 Timothy 1:6), for the purpose fulfillment, edifying the church and glorifying God.
  1. Manifestation of any gifts does not measure spirituality or spiritual maturity. It is the manifestation the fruit of the Spirit that determines the spirituality of the believer.
