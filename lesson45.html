2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 45

Serving the Least

Lesson Starts

Text: Matthew 25

Memory Verse: “When did we see you sick or in prison and go to visit you?' "The King will reply, 'I tell you the truth, whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers of mine, you did for me.' – Matt. 25:39-40

Bottom Line: We are to serve others in the body of Christ.

Lesson Objective: This lesson will help teens to be responsive in reaching to the needy in church.

Lesson Introduction

Have you seen a big guy despised a small guy? What was your conclusion? Did you feel for the small guy, or took sides with the big guy? Probably by now, you have determined NEVER to be the small guy. Friend, we are all humans, and God loves us, that He gave up His all in his Son for our redemption. That God did not introduce a higher, or a more decorated saviour, for the upper class, shows the indiscriminate and benevolent nature our redemption, and this should exactly determine the value we place on one another as human beings, and more importantly as members of the body of Christ. How exactly would you describe Jesus’ redemptive work on earth? It wasn’t about showing that He was a superior being or that He had all the power and could whatever He wanted to do. It was about serving others, “For even the Son of Man did not come to be served (as the big guy), but to serve (as the small guy), and to give his life as a ransom for many” (Mark 10:45). There’s no playing the big guy, if we are going to be part of the Kingdom, service is the way up. This means living our lives for others. When you occupy a saving position, meet a need, positively touch lives, dear you are truly serving.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The parable in our text was part of the discussions Jesus had with His disciples on the Mount of Olives, close to the end of His time on earth. In response to the disciples’ pestering question about the time and signs of His return, Jesus narrated a series of apocalyptic events that will signify the end times (Matt.24). However, Jesus began to direct their attention to a more important concern, which is the kind of attitude they should have in anticipation of His coming. In other words, rather than fretting about when the last days would be, the disciples should be concerned about what their disposition should be in view of His coming. This He did, by narrating four parables to them. This parables illustrate Kingdom principles required for inheriting the kingdom of Christ. First, the parable of the watchful servant (24:45-51), which teaches faithfulness and responsibility; second, the ten virgins 25:1-13), which illustrates preparedness; next is the parable of the talents (vv.14-30), which teaches productivity and lastly the parable of the sheep and goats, which is our focus in this lesson.

Lesson Outline

I. Serving The Least is Serving Christ

Lesson Commentary

I. Serving the least is serving Christ

The grandeur and glory that will accompany the coming of Christ and the details of that event are not our focus in this lesson. However it is important to note that His coming is certain and it will be sudden. He will come as a king to judge the righteous (sheep) and the unrighteous (goats). His judgement would not be based how much knowledge acquired, wealth earned or fame gained. Reward will be given based on service rendered to the “the least of these my brothers and sisters” (v. 40, NLT). The phrase “the least of these brothers and sisters (brethren)” suggest that Jesus wasn’t referring just any poor or needy, but those who are in need in the body of Christ. Like the phrase “one of these little ones” (Matt 10:42; 18:6, 10, 14), Jesus was referring to His disciples—His followers. In other words, this is not just about acts of charity or worldly philanthropy, but about help rendered to those who belong to Christ. What counts here is not just service to others, but service to others that is “unto Him.” This looks like what Paul said to the Galatians, “Therefore, as we have opportunity, let us do good to all people, especially to those who belong to the family of believers” (Gal 6:10) . When Jesus said in helping them, “you were doing it to me”, this filial identification with His disciples, recalls the principle of Matthew 10:40-42, where to receive the disciples is to receive Jesus and it is a cup of water given to “one of these little ones because he is my disciple” that will be rewarded. To serve ‘the least of these’ is to serve Christ.

II. Serving the least is Christlikeness

From the way the judgement was delivered, it would appear that Jesus was promoting works of charity as basis for salvation. Didn’t the Bible teach that “he saved us, not because of the righteous things we had done…” (Titus 3:5) and that “Salvation is not a reward for the good things we have done, so none of us can boast about it” (Eph. 2:9)? In actual sense, the criterion of judgment is mere charity (which isn’t bad after all), but people’s response to the needs of the Kingdom as they have met in those who belong to Jesus. Love and kindness are virtues of Christlikeness as we have learnt in Lesson 7. The judgment of Christ is based on the expression of outward works of love and kindness to who belong to Christ and are suffering— hungry and thirsty, cold and naked (no clothes to wear), homeless, and sick and in prison. The expression of love and compassion is taken as an inherent part of faith and salvation (vv. 35-36). Yes, we are not saved by works, but don’t forget that we are God's workmanship (masterpiece), created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do (Eph. 2:10). Yes, it is not by works of righteousness, but we can’t John’s admonition, “let no one deceive and lead you astray. He who practices righteousness [who is upright, conforming to the divine will in purpose, thought, and action, living a consistently conscientious life] is righteous, even as He is righteous” (1 John 3:7, AMP). Good works do not produce good character; good character produces good works. The proof of righteousness is therefore in the works it produces, just as wisdom is justified by all its works (Lk. 7:35).

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. What truths have you learnt about serving the least?

2. What decisions will you be making in view of what you have learnt?

3. Do a list of “the least” in your local church or around you, decide on what acts of service you would render to at least one of “the least”.

Lesson Summary

  1. In the light of today’s lesson Burton Coffman well observes: “No thoughtful person can conclude that Jesus equated salvation with benevolence in the usual sense. It is not mere charity, but help of Christ's followers that is highlighted here. If this principle were more widely understood and accepted, it would revolutionize [people’s] attitude toward the Church. In the final essence, what [people] do to His Church, they do to Him. To neglect, flout, or dishonour the Church is to do the same to Christ who is the Head of the Church. On the other hand, those who support and provide for the church and extend their concern and constant aid on behalf of her poor and needy, do the same for Christ whose body is the Church.”
  1. Service to others in Christ is redemptive: the love of Christ is expressed by the giver, and experienced by the receiver. Not only is service to others in Christ redemptive it also has eternal consequences. “For the Son of Man is going to come in his Father's glory with his angels, and then he will reward each person according to what he has done” (Matt 16:27).
