2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 22

The Spirit Filled Child (Children’s Day)

Lesson Starts

Text: Ephesians 6:1-3

Memory Verse: "Honor your father and your mother, so that you may live long in the land the Lord your God is giving you.” - Exodus 20:12

“Children, obey your parents in the Lord, for this is right.” - Ephesians 6:1

Bottom Line: Children, teenagers inclusive, must be filled with the Spirit in order to obey and honour their parents.

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson, teens would be able to determine to be who God has created them to be both at home and in the society.

Lesson Introduction

The sinful nature which man inherited from Adam (and Eve) is rebellious, stubborn, irrational, and boastful. When we come to Christ however, the Holy Spirit comes to live in us. As we allow His move and control over our lives, He enables us to obey God, and obey our parents; live disciplined and impactful lives, and thus live daily as great vessels of honour:

1. A source of blessing to our generation and

2. An instrument of praise to God.

The story of Naaman’s Jewish maid whose kind effort led to the cure of the Syrian general’s leprosy remains a great inspiration to us. Have you read that story? It is found in 2 Kings 5. She was a vessel of honour, even in a foreign land. She was an ambassador of Yahweh. What kind of impact, on your fellow mankind – right from home to school, the Church and in fact, everywhere else?

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The Bible enlightens us of the immense benefit of the Holy Spirit in the life of a child of God. When our lives are spirit-filled, we don’t struggle with the will of God. A clear manifestation of the fruit of the Spirit in our lives becomes undeniable. The life of a Spirit is filled child is characterized thus: loves unconditionally; ever joyful and full of peace; not easily provoked or taking vengeance, ever gentle and full of goodness; it is a life of total faith in God trusting Him for all, no matter what; it is meek (submissive, cooperative) and full of temperance (self-control) (Gal. 5:22).

Lesson Outline

I. The Spirit Filled Child Obeys Leadership

II. The Spirit Filled Child Honours Parents

Lesson Commentary

I. The Spirit Filled Child Obeys Leadership

A major characteristic of a Spirit-filled person is obedience—obedience to the leading of the Spirit, obedience to legitimate authority. As teenagers and co-labourers with God in building God’s church and growing the Kingdom through evangelism, we are called to a life of loyalty and obedience to leadership, as opposed to rebellion. We should be submissive and cooperative, so that our impact and relevance to the work is effective and productive. This is why we must be filled with the Holy Spirit. Our loyalty will earn us the listening ears of our leaders and so our suggestions, lofty desires and aspirations will be highly esteemed and incorporated into the large plan of the church for growth in all dimensions to the glory of God. It is required of us to steadily pray for our leaders; it is required of us to share the work with our leaders, making ourselves available to serve at whatever capacity. As we do these, God promotes us from level to level and causes us to be celebrated before men.

II. The Spirit Filled Child Honours Parents

As we have noted in both our introduction and insight, when we are filled with the Holy Spirit, our obedience to God comes with willingness. Honouring our parents (and in fact elders and teachers) is obedience to God’s command, and it brings us honour before God. As we already know, honouring our parents is a secret to living a long rewarding life. When we submit fully to the Spirit of God who lives in us, we allow Him to work out in us such wonderful and gracious virtues that stand us out as well-brought-up children to the praise and glory of God who has called us and made us a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, a holy nation and a peculiar (special) people (1 Pet. 2:9).

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. What is our proof of infilling with the Holy Spirit?

2. How does being filled with the Spirit profit us?

3. In what ways can teens make themselves sufficiently relevant to God’s work in the Church?

Lesson Summary

1. Disrespect to authority, especially by young people is a growing concern in our society today. There is serious concern on how children and adolescents treat their parents and elders. This is a sign that we are in the last days (2 Tim. 3:2). The spirit of the last days is at work. However, a Spirit-life can’t be overcome by this spirit.

2. The hallmark of a Spirit-filled life is obedience—obedience to God and to legitimate authority. Church leadership and parents are major legitimate authorities in the life of a Spirit-filled teenager.

3. When we submit fully to the Spirit of God who lives in us, we allow Him to work out in us such wonderful and gracious virtues that stand us out as well-brought-up children to the praise and glory of God.
