2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 29

Saul Begins to Preach

Lesson Starts

Text: Acts 9:19b-31

Memory Verse: “And after taking some food, he regained his strength. Saul spent several days with the disciples in Damascus. At once he began to preach in the synagogues that Jesus is the Son of God.”

Acts 9:19-20

Bottom Line: We are to tell others about the gospel of Jesus Christ as soon as we get saved.

Lesson Objective: This lesson will encourage the teens to preach Jesus Christ as the Son of God and Savior of the world.

Lesson Introduction

Anani grew up in the Middle East learning to wage jihad against all non-Muslims, especially Jews and Christians. For years, he did that. Anani belonged to several Islamic terrorist groups in Lebanon. By 14, he had already committed his first murder, and within four years, he had 223 kills to his credit. Amazingly, the same Christian faith that Anani had wanted to crush eventually changed his life. After a chance encounter with a Christian preacher on a street in Lebanon, Anani left Islam and terrorism at the age of 17. He has now devoted his life to speaking out against radical Islam and standing up for Christ. His family reacted by disowning him. His father shredded his first Bible, and slapped him severally. Outside his home, things were even worse. He was dragged to the local mosque, beaten and excommunicated. There were also several attempts on his life. But Anani refused to be intimidated or be swayed from his new faith. Anani now lives in North America, where he is director of evangelism for an International ministry. His time in the West hasn’t always gone smoothly either. He has been assaulted and seen his life threatened by Muslims in the United States and Canada. Anani’s story seems to be a re-enactment of Saul of Tarsus’ conversion in our text.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The conversion of Saul of Tarsus, the leading persecutor of the Christians in the early church, was perhaps the greatest event in church history after the coming of the Spirit at Pentecost. Saul met Christ in a unique encounter on his way to Damascus where he was headed to legally persecute the Christians in that city. While his Jewish allies were piqued by his conversion to Christianity and attempted to kill him, his conversion story was met with scepticism, among the Christians, who couldn’t come to terms with the fact that the number one Enemy of the Church was now a changed person, and a professed Christian at that. “It must definitely be a set up”, they must have thought.

Lesson Outline

I. The Persecutor Becomes A Preacher

II. The Persecutor Becomes The Persecuted

III. The Persecutor Becomes Proven

Lesson Commentary

I. The Persecutor Becomes A Preacher

Saul wasted no time in transferring his former zeal into his new found faith, realising how much time he had wasted fighting Christ in the name of religion. Shortly after his conversion, he began to testify of the same Christ he once persecuted to everyone’s amazement. The question on the people’s lips was “isn’t he the man who wreaked havoc in Jerusalem among those who call on this name? And hasn’t he come here to take them as prisoners to the chief priests?

II. The Persecutor Becomes The Persecuted

What goes around comes around. Saul the persecutor now becomes the persecuted. Saul’s unwavering boldness at preaching the gospel was unwelcomed by his fellow unbelieving Jews who see Christians as blasphemers. They planned to kill Saul but his new friends and followers helped him to escape safely out of Damascus and went back to Jerusalem .

III. The Persecutor Becomes Proven

Back in Jerusalem, Saul had a very poor reception from the believers there, because they knew who he was and the mission that took him out of Jerusalem to Damascus. It was unimaginable to assume such a high profile “terrorist” was now a believer. Saul needed someone to speak for him so he could be accepted into the believer’s fold and Barnabas (as his name implies, son of consolation) came to his rescue. Having won the confidence of the Christian fold, Saul at once began to preach again. Persecution arose once again from the Grecian Jews to kill him but he was helped by his fellow believers to escape to his home town, Tarsus.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. What parallels can you draw from the story of Anani and the life of Saul?

2. What is the significance of Barnabas’ role in helping Saul settle into the Christian community?

3. What risks have you taken in sharing your faith?

Lesson Summary

There was an urgency in the way Saul handled his new found faith. He wasted no time to make known that Jesus Christ is the Son of God and Savior of the whole world. As teenagers we should be pretty excited about sharing our faith with others, just as we should be aware that our faith will be tested. Persecution may come either from within or outside, but we must continue to proclaim Jesus as Lord and Savior. Also, we must understand that there is no sinner so bad, who God cannot save. There’s always pardon from Jesus for the even the vilest offender who truly believes. Saul and Anani are living testimonies to this.
