2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 24

Grateful Faith

Lesson Starts

Text: Luke 17:11-19

Memory Verse: “ Then he said to him, "Rise and go; your faith has made you well." - Luke 17:19

Bottom Line: Appreciation is a key to wholeness

Lesson Objective: This lesson will help the teens to be more appreciative for either small or big things

Lesson Introduction

If we realise that it is by God’s grace that we are saved and we cannot amount to anything without Him, then we would with ease, exercise towards Him a grateful faith. A grateful faith keeps us in the arena of God’s favour. A grateful faith is desired of every child of God.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

Jesus was on His way to Jerusalem. He passed through Samaria and Galilee and then He entered a certain village where ten lepers met Him. The lepers called out to Him and asked for His mercy. Jesus in turn asked them to go and show themselves to the priests according to the law (cf. Leviticus 14). They were on their way to the priest when they were all cleansed of their leprosy. One of them (a Samaritan) immediately returned to Jesus to give Him thanks and glorified God.

Lesson Outline

I. Ten Lepers Cleansed

II. One Leper Returned with thanks

Lesson Commentary

I. Ten Lepers Cleansed

Jesus was met by ten lepers in a certain village as he journeyed through Samaria and Galilee to Jerusalem. The lepers cry to him for mercy and he directed them to present themselves to the priests as the law for cleansing of lepers prescribes. They obeyed Jesus and were on their way when they received their miracle of cleansing. Obedience to Jesus is no doubt, key to the cleansing of the lepers. Let us take note of it.

II. One Leper Returns with thanks

Immediately one of the ten lepers received his healing, he returned to Jesus and with a loud voice, he glorified God. Verse 16 of our text says this man fell down on his face at the feet of Jesus and gave Him thanks, talking about gratitude from the depth of one’s heart. Jesus was honoured by the Samaritan’s gesture and so he asked: “Were there not ten cleansed? But where are the nine?” It is a good thing to be grateful and give God thanks in all things (1 Thes. 5:18). Giving thanks to God always is an expression of faith in Him. To the grateful Samaritan, Jesus said, “Arise, go thy way: thy faith hath made thee whole.” Let us learn to be thankful to God in all things – small or big – so we can stay whole always.

Discussion Questions / Activities

1. What could be the reason(s) why the other nine lepers failed to return to give thanks? How should we apply this to ourselves?

2. Why is it good to give thanks to God in all things?

3. How can we cultivate the attitude of gratitude?

Lesson Summary

We are living in a generation when young people are said to have become increasingly ungrateful. Teenagers wonder what their parents have done for them. If they can’t have what other children are having, then their parents are not doing enough. They the world is all about them, they feel a sense of entitlement just about anything. Charitable values have given way to self-centredness and greed in a throwaway society. This is not the life we have been called into. Learning and living out the value of gratitude is the way of faith. This faith teaches that we are product of grace, we nothing in ourselves except by the grace of God (1Cor. 15:10). There is nothing we are or have that we have not received (1 Cor. 4:7), so we have every reason to be grateful to God to those He has put in care of our lives. We should be grateful to God in all things – be they small or big. God desires it so. This is the path to wholeness.

Anticipating the next lesson

Assignment

Be deliberately grateful to family members / schoolmates and friends even for very little help or support. Note and record their responses for class discussion.
