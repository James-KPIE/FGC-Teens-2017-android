2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 30

The United Family

Lesson Starts

Text: Ephesians 5:21-6:4

Memory Verse: “Children, obey your parents in the Lord, for this is right. "Honour your father and mother"-which is the first commandment with a promise— "that it may go well with you and that you may enjoy long life on the earth." - Ephesians 6:1-3

Bottom Line: Family, a unit of mankind where everybody belongs should bond together regardless of circumstances.

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson, teens would appreciate their family unit regardless of status.

Lesson Introduction

The institution of the family (a man, woman, and their children) is not merely an invention of the society, but an institution founded by God himself. It is God’s agency for populating the earth with a godly generation (Mal. 2:15). The family is formed exclusively through a loving lifelong marriage covenant between a man and a woman. The traditional family was once the core foundation and stabilizing force of the society. However, the family has become so fragmented that it is difficult to describe a normal family in a society that is lapsing into degeneracy. With the high rate of divorce, children being born out of wedlock, homosexual marriages, sexual relationships outside of marriage becoming the norm, and other sexual and marital distortions, the family is being redefined by those who think little or nothing of the integrity of the institution. There are no scruples. Certainly, in a society where there are no boundaries, the definition of morality becomes relative—depending on what fits the situation. Sadly, Christian families are being soiled and mired in this moral erosion. There is increasing concern about the lack of spiritual focus in the homes of many Christians. Family altars and Bible studies have given way to addictive television viewing, entertainment, and other activities that threaten the home. This shift has had a distressing impact on the collective values of today’s Christian family. Nevertheless, the foundation of God stands sure, the Bible remains the manual for the principles that holds the family together.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

Right from Eden God set the paradigm for the family. The family was supposed to take after the Godhead, God Himself being a family as manifested in the Trinity and in His capacity as the Creator, being the Father of all creation (Eph. 3:14-15). Subsequent to the fall, sin led to the distortion of this divine institution. The family became a showground for the struggle between husbands, wives and children. While husbands resort to dominating their wives, wives contrive in manipulating their husbands. Parents mistreat or undermine their children, while the children disobey and dishonour their parents. Alluding to foundational Old Testament texts in Matthew 19:6, Jesus asserted the unity that should characterize the family, making it clear, that whatever anomalies that may have altered the family institution, was never part of God’s original intention (Matt. 19:8). Paul’s treatment of the issue of the family in our text must be studied in the entire context of the letter to the Ephesians. Marriage is set in the larger context of the restoration of all things and the unity of the body of Christ (the Church) under the headship of Christ. Christ’s relationship with the Church, provides the pattern for Christian marriage and by implication, Christian family. Therefore, the Spirit of Christ must influence all family relationships (v. 18). Family members who are Spirit-filled will invariably uphold and adhere to God’s principles for the family.

Lesson Commentary

I. The Principle of Authority

Family members must acknowledge that God is the Source of all authority. He has all power and all authority, and He entrusts roles of leadership to individuals in the family. All the family members are persons equal in dignity. However, family members must subscribe to the lines of authority God has set in the family. First, the orderliness in structures of authority reflects the order of God’s own nature. God is a Trinity: the Father, the Son Jesus Christ, and the Holy Spirit. Each member of the Trinity works within the structure of authority and fulfils a specific role, perfectly complementing the others and demonstrating God’s glory. Second, God outlines authority structures that provide direction and functionality for the family. The principle of authority, requires that since God placed authorities over us, to obey them is to submit to God’s design and authority in our lives. Authority comes with responsibility, both for the one who exercises authority and the one who is under authority. God assigns husbands with the responsibility of the leadership of the family unit. A husband is to love his wife as Christ loves the Church. The metaphor of giving up oneself for another as Christ did, indicates the extreme devotion and selflessness with which the husband must understand, care for, protect and provide for the needs (domestic and spiritual) of the wife—and of course the family. In serving the needs of the family, the husband must put aside his own interests. A wife is to submit to the leadership of her husband, with respect, meekness and as a helper, homemaker and a faithful collaborator in building a godly home. Parents are responsible to teach their children the truth of God’s word, train them in basic life skills, and discipline them in love, when their heart stray in folly; and children are to honour and obey their parents. The injunction to honour one’s parents entails respect for both father and mother. The injunction has no limitation or termination, that is, no one outgrows honouring one’s parents. It is also noteworthy that the injunction comes with divine blessings and long life (6:3).

II. The Principle of Accountability

Family members must acknowledge that everyone is accountable to God in carrying out their responsibilities in the family. Each person is accountable to God for his or her actions, whether he is in authority over others or in a position under authority. Authority figures are accountable for how they exercise their authority. Those under authority are also accountable to God for their responses to authority. In view of this truth, it behoves family members to be accountable to one another, in dispensing their roles and responsibilities in the family. For, in being accountable to one another they are being accountable to God. Family members cannot think that the observance of their responsibilities is conditional to the response of the others. In other words, the husband should not think that his obligation to love his wife should be in reciprocity to his wife showing him respect and vice versa. Children cannot chose to respect their parents, contingent upon whether or not they are deserving of respect, just as parents cannot deny the child of care and nurture, because of misbehaviour. Family members must realize that the recompense of obedience or otherwise of this principle is with God and not with their human wardens or wards.

III. The Principle of Amity

Family relationships must be built on the platform of mutual affection and goodwill for one another. Affection and goodwill are the outgrowth of love, motivated by the Holy Spirit (Rom. 5:5). When love is at the centre of family relationships, we would treat one another with respect and understanding and we would appreciate one another’s uniqueness. Love motivates us to be conciliatory in accommodating one another’s shortcomings and in building support systems that will grow and solidify our family relationships. That is love motivated by the Spirit in us in not a sentimental love, rather it is volitional, because it derives from the same love with which Christ loves us. Christ loves us ‘in spite of’ not ‘because of’. Christ chose to love us irrespective of our foibles, family relationships must be based on love without preconditions.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. How significant are the principles discussed in this study to your role or responsibility in your family?

2. Read Acts 4:19 and Colossians 3:23-24, how should we respond under an authority that asks us to do something that is wrong?

3. What kind of parent would like to be and what kind of disposition would expect your children to have towards you, when become a parent?

Lesson Summary

The disinclination of children obey or honour their parents has always been a matter of concern, ever since Paul’s days. He warned Timothy, “For men will be lovers of self, lovers of money, boastful, arrogant, revellers, disobedient to parents, ungrateful, unholy…” (2 Tim. 3:2). Peter also corroborates Paul’s teaching when he warned about God’s anger against self-willed renegades who will disregard authority, thus refusing to give honour to those whom it is due (2 Pet. 2:10). In the face of the rising tide of relativism, it is almost becoming cheesy to talk about obeying parents. Children are unnecessarily indulged or left on their own, with little or no parental authority or influence over their lives. This is a major part of the dysfunctionality that has impaired the family institution. However, popular culture does not change the standards of God. As a teenager planted in a family, it is significant that you stand out in upholding and modelling Biblical principles in your relationship with your parents or wardens.
