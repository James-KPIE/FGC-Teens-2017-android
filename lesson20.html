2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 20

Honour Your Mother (Mothers’ Day)

Lesson Starts

Text: Exo. 20:12; Pro. 31:25-31; Jn. 19:25-27; Eph. 16:1-

3; Col. 3:20

Memory Verse: "Honor your father and your mother, so that you may live long in the land the Lord your God is giving you.” - Exodus 20:12

Bottom Line: If anyone dishonours or disrespects his or her mother, he or she will not be greatly blessed

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson, teens will have more respect and honour for their parents

Lesson Introduction

God’s word is all the treasure that we ever need to lead our lives successfully. Our memory verse for this lesson, which is one of the Ten Commandments God gave to His chosen people enjoins us to honour our parents, so that our lives may not be cut short abruptly, but be prolonged unto good old age. This is a lesson every teenager must take heed of, to safeguard his or her life.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The children of Israel had just been freed by God from oppression and slavery in Egypt. They had miraculously crossed the Red Sea on dry land and were in the wilderness travelling towards Canaan, the land of promise. God decided to give to them guiding principles – these are the Ten Commandments – to guide their conduct in their relationship with God; with one another and with the strangers on the way and in the land of Canaan. The Ten Commandments summarized God’s moral law for Israel and described their obligations both to Him and to others. Jesus Christ and the apostles affirmed that the commandments are an expression of God’s holy will and thus remain obligatory for New Testament believers (Matt.22:37-39; Mk.12:28-34; Lk.10:27; Rom.13:9-10; Gal.5:14).

Lesson Outline

I. GOD COMMANDS HONOUR TO MOTHERS

II. GOD REWARDS HONOUR TO MOTHERS

Lesson Commentary

I. God Commands Honour to Mothers

As we see in our texts for this lesson, honouring our mothers/parents is God’s command to His children. Just as it was important for the children of Israel to obey this commandment, so it is to us, to obey this commandment today. The Bible teaches that we are to give due care, regard, and attention to our parents and be helpful to them. Are there ways you dishonor you parents? Think deeply, remember them and walk away from them – forever. As the world celebrates Mothers’ Day, what pleasant surprise would you give to your own mother? A card with great poetry? A hug and a song of appreciation for being such a caring mother? A gift no matter how little? An unexpected text message? Think of what to do and do it.

II. God Rewards Honour to Mothers

Who is that teenager whose one major desire is to truncate his or her destiny by deliberately cutting short his or her life? Certainly, we don’t have such a person among us. We please God when we honour our parents, and He in turn, lengthens our lifespan (Col. 3:20; Ex. 20:12; Eph. 6:1-3). Imagine how much you would make God happy when you pleasantly surprise your mother continually with little praise and tokens of appreciation here and there, especially as she celebrates one landmark or the other in her life; imagine how God would be pleased as you daily shower her love and affection, as your statement of honour and deep respect for her; your demonstration of unalloyed appreciation of her valuable influence upon your life. God says when you do this, you earn for yourself, long useful life.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. In what ways can we as teenagers honour our parents?

2. How do you particularly honour your own mother?

3. What could be causes of disrespect to parents among teenagers? How should we deal with such causes?

Lesson Summary

God demands that teenagers honour their mothers/parents at all times. This is the secret to living a long rewarding life.

Teacher

Think of any special beautiful way to honour your mother. Go ahead and do it and observe how she responds for class testimony/discussion.
