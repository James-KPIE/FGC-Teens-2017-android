2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 42

Sharing with Sincerity

Lesson Starts

Text: Acts 4:34-5:10

Memory Verse: “Each man should give what he has decided in his heart to give, not reluctantly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver.” - 2 Corinthians 9:7

Bottom Line: We must be sincere with the message in sharing and preaching of the gospel.

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson, teens should be able to share their love, gifts and endowment with others as they preach the gospel to them.

Lesson Introduction

All that surround the birth, death, burial and resurrection of our Lord Jesus Christ are not only unique but transcend the ordinary. Hundred percent God, born hundred percent man, died hundred percent man and rose hundred percent God. Jesus gave His life to humanity that we might have life in abundance (Jn. 10:10).

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

So much had happened since the descent of the Holy Spirit at Pentecost. The apostles were still basking in the euphoria of the Holy Ghost. The church was increasing daily in multiple thousands. Miracles and healings were being wrought by the hands of the apostles. The bond of fellowship was growing stronger and more intimate. The apostolic grace was waxing stronger. The Gospel was spreading wider and the wave of the new move was the talk of the town. The religious leaders of the day became uncomfortable and unleashed persecution on the Church. They targeted their leaders (Peter and John), arrested and imprisoned them. But persecution only strengthened their unity and bolstered their confidence. Eventually, Peter and John were released, with threats from the Jewish leaders, as they couldn’t find anything on them. The church resisted the threats of the authorities with passionate and powerful corporate prayers. This sparked another bout of revival with the release of so much grace, as the apostles continued to proclaim Christ with increased fervour.

Lesson Outline

I. Sharing with Grace

II. Sharing with Greed

Lesson Commentary

I. Sharing with Grace

With increased grace, came increased power, and with an increased power, came an increased church, with an increased church, increased needs. However, as the needs increased, love also increased to cater for those needs. The church had prayed for a boldness in their profession of the gospel, and God graciously granted their request. But the boldness of the church in its profession was also practiced with regard to its possessions. What they were doing wasn’t new, because that was how they started when the church was born in Acts 2. The difference here was that the church had become larger and as such, the great grace unleashed by the Spirit motivated the saints to go the extra mile in meeting the needs of others in the fold. Further, earlier they had sold their goods as needs arose and personally met them. But now, with a much larger church and more needs, they sold their possessions and brought the money to the apostles, leaving it to them to administer the funds. It is much easier to be motivated to give to one’s neighbour, whose needs are immediately evident to us, than it is to give to those we do not even know. There had to have been a high level of confidence in the apostles for the money to have been laid at their feet. This kind of disposition wasn’t just a function of sheer generosity, it must have been motivated by the Holy Spirit. A notable example of such bigheartedness, was Barnabas (4:36), who would later become Paul’s missionary companion. He sold a piece of land he owned in Cyprus and brought the proceeds from the sale to the Apostles.

II. Sharing with Greed

The notorious couple, in our story, Ananias and Sapphira, were not as gracious as Barnabas. Indeed they did sell their land just like some others had done, but they conspired to deceive the Church. They decided that they would some of the proceeds of the sale to the apostles, and keep back a small amount for themselves. They lied about the amount they gave. They made it look like they were giving the entire proceeds from the sale. They wanted to make an impression. In the real sense they didn’t have to lie or be deceitful. It wasn’t as if they were under compulsion to give a particular amount. It was their decision to sell the land and donate the sale to the welfare purse of the church. They could have been sincere and transparent about what they wanted to give. If they didn’t want to give everything, it wouldn’t have been a problem with the apostles. But keeping part of the money, and coming to show off as if they were giving everything, was a duplicitous behaviour. They didn’t just offend the apostles or the church, they actually sinned against the Holy Spirit. Peter, who probably by discernment, knew what the couple had done, confronted the husband, “How is it that Satan has so filled your heart that you have lied to the Holy Spirit” (Acts 5:3). Ananias immediately dropped dead. His wife showed up three hours later and Peter confronted her as well. She had the chance to repent, but she chose keep up with the lie. Peter then pointed out that she had conspired with Ananias against the Lord and would share his fate, at which point Sapphira fell and died (Acts 5:10). As the grace of God increased, so did His power and glory. The greater His grace in our lives, the more of His fear (reverence) we should have in our lives. In the hymn, Amazing Grace, John Newton wrote, “Twas grace that taught my heart to fear.” Ananias and Sapphira, took the grace of God for granted and they paid the price of their hypocrisy.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. What has God given us that He expects us to give back?

2. Humanly speaking, is it really possible to give without expecting something back in return?

3. Discuss how and what we can sincerely share with others in Christ Jesus without being carnal?

Lesson Summary

1. There’s so much hypocrisy going on in the church today. People like to put up appearances. They seek for cheap popularity and vain glory on the platform of deception and phony piety. This was exactly what Ananias and Sapphira typified.

2. Barnabas’ sacrifice and labour of love genuinely demonstrate what should motivate our giving to meet the needs of others in the body Christ. There was no trace of fleshly pride in what he did. His only thought was the need of other Christians and the glory of God.

3. Our acts of love, should not be done to impress people, patronize church leadership or score a point. We should be motivated by sincere love and faith in God the Rewarder (Heb.11:6), knowing fully well that “God is not unjust; he will not forget your work and the love you have shown him as you have helped his people and continue to help them” (Heb. 6:10).
