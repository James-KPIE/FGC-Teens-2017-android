2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 53

Worship the Son of God

Lesson Starts

Text: Matthew 14:22-36

Memory Verse: “In the past God spoke to our forefathers through the prophets at many times and in various ways, but in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son, whom he appointed heir of all things, and through whom he made the universe. The Son is the radiance of God's glory and the exact representation of his being, sustaining all things by his powerful word. After he had provided purification for sins, he sat down at the right hand of the Majesty in heaven.” – Heb. 1:1-3

Bottom Line: Jesus Christ is the only begotten Son of God.

Lesson Objective : At the end of this lesson, teens will

appreciate Jesus’ sacrifice on the cross and thereby worship Him with all they are and have.

Lesson Introduction

The English word worship comes from the Old English word worship, a word which denotes the worthiness of the one receiving the special honour or devotion. This connotes actions motivated by an attitude that reveres, honours, or describes the worth of another person or object. In Biblical context worship refers to reverent devotion and service to God motivated by God's saving acts in history. In our text (v.32), like in the other Synoptic Gospels, the word worship connotes an inner attitude of adoration, respect, and humility spontaneously expressed in a physical manner. Worship, is therefore a celebration of God in His supreme worth in such a manner that His worthiness becomes the norm and inspiration of human existence. This was exactly the understanding Paul tried establish to at the meeting of the Areopagus in Athens (Acts 17:22-29). In spite of their religiosity, the Athenians were ignorant of the true God. Paul was saying to them that their object of worship shouldn’t be in their pantheon of gods, represented by statues of stone, silver and gold, but the true God, who is at the centre of their existence, “For in Him we live and move and have our being…We are His children” (Acts 17:28).

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

Following the reports of John the Baptist’s death from his disciples, Jesus decided to withdraw into solitude. The ministry of Jesus was already gathering momentum and the crowd was seriously on his trail. They saw in Jesus a prophet, even more than a prophet, more like a political liberator, who will deliver them from Roman rule, and so they sought Him out with the agenda of making Him king (John 6:14-15). Although Jesus didn’t share their political view, He couldn’t help but to be moved with compassion, when He saw the mammoth crowd. Jesus healed their sick as He had always done, but gave them a reason to stretch their imagination, when at nightfall, instead of sending them away, decided to feed them with only five loaves and two fish. Five thousand men, apart from women and children—that was some number, and when they were done, there were still leftovers of 12 baskets. Now, more than ever before these guys were certain, this was the king they had been waiting for. But found a way to dismiss them (v.23), before they start getting any ideas.

Lesson Commentary

I. The Worthiness of The Son

The public miracle of Jesus feeding the multitude was now followed by the one witnessed only by the disciples— He literarily walked on water, amid a turbulent storm, He saved Peter from sinking and then He calmed the storm. Wow! This was much more than they could take in. Perhaps they had thought, like John disciples that Jesus was going be John’s successor (reporting John’s death to Jesus indicated that John’s disciples assumed and recognised Jesus as John’s true successor) — this was way above that. They might have even shared the sentiments of the multitude that Jesus was a prophet and a liberator-king, but again, what they witnessed exceeded that. Jesus was more than a prophet, more than a king— when He calmed the first storm, they had marvelled in the interrogative, “What manner of Man is this?” (Matt 8:23-27) But now their clear testimony was in the affirmative, “Truly you are the Son of God” (v.33). It was upon this proclamation that they worshipped Jesus. The combined effect of Jesus’ miracle exploits demonstrated His authority over material things and led to a new appreciation of Him being more than human, and therefore worthy of worship. They didn’t just acknowledge His worthiness as a superman, but as Divine. The title the “Son of God” is a designation of His Godhood. It denotes His equality with the Father. To call Christ the Son of God is to assert His true Divinity: The second Person of the Trinity.

II. The Worship of The Son

Having acknowledged Jesus worthiness they worshiped Him. This means worship in every sense of the word as highlighted in the introduction of this lesson. Their act of worship involved the totality of their being. First, it involved their intellect. They worshipped Jesus based on the knowledge they had of Him. This knowledge was what motivated the worth they attributed to Him and acknowledged Him as Deity. They weren’t just following a routine or observing some kind of mysterious ritual. They knew the object of their worship and why they were worshipping Him. Jesus said to the Samaritan woman, “You Samaritans worship what you do not know” (John 4:22). This wasn’t the case for the disciples. Second, their worship involved their emotion. That was why it was spontaneous, it wasn’t mechanical, or by coercion. It was an act of delight, not of duty. The knowledge they had of Jesus, inspired a feeling of awe (an overwhelming feeling of reverence and admiration, produced by that which is grand, sublime or extremely powerful) and joy. That was why they expressed reverence and devotion towards Him in worship. There was also a physical aspect to their worship. They paid homage to Him, like bowing down, or genuflecting. This is a gestural side to worship. Those physical gestures were an outward expression of what they were feeling inside.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. What are some the truths you’ve learnt about worshiping the Son?

2. How would that affect your attitude to worship henceforth?

3. What could possibly contest with the worship the Son in our lives?

Lesson Summary

  1. If the disciples acknowledge and worshipped Jesus as God, because His exploits, we have even greater reasons, to worship Him today.
  2. Our memory verse (Heb.1:2-3) helps us to appreciate the why we have more reason to worship.
    1. “By his Son, God created the world in the beginning, and it will all belong to the Son at the end. This Son perfectly mirrors God, and is stamped with God's nature.
    2. He regulates the universe by the mighty power of his command.
    3. He is the one who died to cleanse us and clear our record of all sin, and then sat down in highest honour beside the great God of heaven”
  3. In the early church the emphasis worship falls on the historical acts of the triune God motivated by infinite love, with the central focus on the incarnation, the cross, and exaltation of Jesus Christ the Lord, made real by the Holy Spirit (Eph. 1:3-14; 2:18). Apostolic worship was a joyous experience that was informed by of glad access to God rather than uncertainty and dread in His presence. This understanding should form our pattern of worship today.
