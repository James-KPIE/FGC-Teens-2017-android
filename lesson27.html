2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 27

Standing Firm Against Opposition

Lesson Starts

Text: Acts 7:1-53

Memory Verse: “And rescued him from all his troubles. He gave Joseph wisdom and enabled him to gain the goodwill of Pharaoh king of Egypt; so he made him ruler over Egypt and all his palace.” - Acts 7:10

Bottom Line: The truth cannot be covered for long

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson, teens will be able to stand firm for their faith in Christ Jesus even in the face of opposition.

Lesson Introduction

Rosa Louise McCauley Parks (1913-2005) was an African American female activist who in 1955 refused to continue to accept the status quo of racism. She refused to give up her seat for a white commuter. This led to her arrest, she lost her job and even got death threats but she refused to let go of her conviction, she stood for equal rights for all men. Parks played an important role in raising international awareness of the plight of African Americans and the civil rights struggle. Parks doggedness and that of other African Americans like Martin Luther King Junior who believed in equality has become a reality. Today in America, an African American is the president. The story of Rosa Parks resonates with the story of Stephen in our text, as they both stuck to their convictions in the face of life-threatening oppositions.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

At the time of Stephen’s accusation, Christianity was a new religion in town, bringing in a new perception to the religious beliefs (Judaism) that they have tenaciously held on to for so long. It was natural for them to have refuted this new way of life as human nature fights what it does not understand. Human nature fights truth. But the custodians of the truth must be willing to present it knowing fully well that truth always prevail over lies. It is said that truth is bitter, nevertheless, it must be told especially, a life changing truth like the gospel of Christ that has the power to transform lives.

Lesson Commentary

I. Stephen’s Accusation

There are three things the Jews hold in high esteem; their religion (Judaism), their land and their temple. The accusers of Stephen brilliantly set him up against the Jewish religious ruling council. Stephen was falsely accused of making derogatory statements against the temple, the land and the laws of Moses; these were serious crimes before the Jewish rulers who constituted the highest religious and political council of their day.

II. Stephen’s Argument

Stephen in response to the accusation rhetorically and logically presented the gospel to his audience. He started from how God called Abraham, the father of faith up until Jesus Christ. He systematically started with what the people already knew to the new faith in Christ they are now violently opposing. In the face of opposition, Stephen’s sound knowledge of the scriptures (Old Testament as was available to them then) came into bear. Biblical history says out of the 40 Old Testament references used in the Book of Acts, Stephen used 15 in this speech. His argument exposed their religiosity and presented the truth which they ignorantly refuted.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. Do you think the Christian faith is worth dying for? (give a good reason for your answer)

2. In this 21st Century, what are the likely oppositions to the Gospel?

3. How then should Christians prepare for and engage opposition?

Lesson Summary

In Stephen’s defence against his accusers, he narrated how Joseph’s conviction about God’s plan for his life attracted his brother’s hatred, envy and anger. They sold him into slavery in order to silence the truth about his future but Joseph held on to his faith in God in spite of slavery, and even in the prison experience, he never denied God. God showed up for him as he eventually became the prime minister of the land of Egypt. Oppositions can come either from within (the home front) or from outside, the sound knowledge of God’s words and promises coupled with strong convictions avail one the boldness to remain firm as demonstrated in the lives of Stephen and even Joseph who was sold into slavery as a teenager. Your knowledge and understanding of God’s word and your commitment to it is essential in standing firm against any opposition to your faith.
