Who Is The Holy Spirit?

Text: John 14:16-17,25-26; 15:26; 16:12-15;Matt. 28:19, Gen.1:2;1:26;

Memory Verse: “Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit” - Matthew 28:19

Bottom Line: The Holy Spirit is a part of the Godhead, equal with the Father and the Son because He is God.

Lesson Objective: In this lesson teens will learn who the Holy Spirit is. They will understand His personality, attributes and power.

Lesson Introduction

The Holy Spirit is both God and a Person just as Jesus and God the Father are. Together the three Persons are the Trinity. This lesson introduces the Holy Spirit. The Holy Spirit is not an abstract concept, a faceless force, or an “it.” He is a Person, just as God the Father and Jesus the Son are Persons. Your task as the teacher, with God’s help, is to help the teens discover the Holy Spirit as a Person who is God. The Trinity and the personhood of the Holy Spirit may be hard to grasp. Your goal is to make this important teaching more concrete so the teens can begin to grasp it. What better way to approach it than as an investigation? Just as forensic scientists seek for the truth, through this session you and your teens will seek for truth about the Holy Spirit.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The Bible does not explain, but it does most certainly reveal the Trinity of the Godhead. It teaches that there is but one God, yet He has chosen to manifest Himself in three distinct personalities. The Holy Spirit is a personality distinct from the Father and Son. But it is still more difficult for many to visualize the Holy Spirit as a definite personality.

So many speak of the Spirit merely as an influence or a power, a manifestation of the divine nature, an agent rather than a person. So often He is spoken of as “it.” So many pray for the power of the Spirit, asking God to send “it” into their midst. This should never be. The Spirit is not merely an influence or a figure of speech. The Holy Spirit is a definite, distinct personality. God will give us insight to a clearer understanding about the Trinity in this lesson.

Suggestion for Teachers Illustration

Use a boiled egg or three entwined rings, probably with different colors.

Although it may not be a perfect representation, yet it can lead you and your students closer to near understanding.

1. God is one complete being in three distinct persons:

2. Father, Son and Holy Spirit.

3. God is three persons in One: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

4. Each one is fully God: God the Father, God the Son, and God the Holy Spirit.

5. There are however not three Gods.

6. The Bible says that there is only one true God (Deut. 6:4).

7. Just as there are not three eggs, there is only one.

8. And yet there are three parts that make up this object.

9. Allow some time for the teens to reflect on this truth through your illustration.

10. Emphasize the limit of human understanding.

11. Lead them to pray as follows:

“Lord, help us to understand who You are more and more every day. You are an Awesome God and we desire to know You more! In Jesus name, Amen!”
