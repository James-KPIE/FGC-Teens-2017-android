Lesson 23

God Gives the Holy Spirit

Lesson Starts

Text: Acts 2:1-21

Memory Verse: “And afterward, I will pour out my Spirit on all people. Your sons and daughters will prophesy, your old men will dream dreams, your young men will see visions.” - Joel 2:28

Bottom Line: The power of the Holy Spirit comes upon those who wait on Him and upon earnest seekers.

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson, teens will have the opportunity of being baptized by the Holy Ghost with initial evidence of speaking with new tongues.

Lesson Introduction

Pentecost Sunday was the day Jesus Christ baptized His early disciples with the Holy Ghost in Jerusalem. The experience was the Holy Spirit coming and resting upon the disciples as tongues of fire, and they all began to speak with strange (new) tongues – languages they never knew or could speak before. Upon this baptism, the disciples received power to boldly preach the gospel of Jesus Christ first in Jerusalem, then all over Judea, and on to Samaria, and to the larger world (Acts 1:8, 2), and to live bold, selfless and fruitful Christian life thereafter. The apostle, beginning with Peter, preached the baptism of the Holy Ghost everywhere they went witnessing Christ. That same baptism has been available to all Christians since then till this day. You too can receive Him and begin to live a bold, selfless, fruitful and profitable Christian life to the glory of God. All you need is to sincerely desire Him and earnestly ask God to do it in your life. He would do it for you.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The feast of Pentecost was one of the major Jewish annual festivals. It was also called the feast of Weeks (Exo. 34:22, Deut. 16:10, 16; 2 Chro. 8:13). It was celebrated fifty days after the feast of Passover. Hence it was called Pentecost. The feast celebrated at the end of grain harvest (Exo. 23:16), when the first-fruits of harvest were presented to God (Lev. 23:17). The feast of Pentecost came by again not long after Jesus had ascended into heaven, having told His disciples (about 120 of them) to gather in Jerusalem and wait for the baptism of the Holy Ghost. It was that day that the Lord chose to baptize His disciples with the Holy Ghost, thereby fulfilling Prophet Joel’s prophecy in Joel 2:28-29. “And it shall come to pass afterward that I will pour out My Spirit upon all flesh and your sons and you daughters shall prophesy, your old men shall dream dreams, your young men shall see visions. And also upon the servants and upon the handmaids in those days will I pour out My Spirit.” The event is vividly recorded in Acts 2. Pentecost symbolizes for the Church, the beginning of God’s harvest of souls in the world.

Lesson Commentary

I. The Holy Spirit Come The Disciples

The disciples had gather (in the upper room) in Jerusalem as Jesus commanded them to do; they were in unison expectant of the “…promise of the Father” (Acts 1:4) (the baptism of the Holy Ghost) as they prayed and waited. Then suddenly, a rushing wind from heaven filled the house where they were with a sound and cloven tongue like fire sat upon each of them – this was the baptism of the Holy Spirit – they were all filled with the Holy Spirit and they began to speak with other tongues as the Spirit gave them utterances (they began to speak new languages that the Spirit enabled them to speak. Please take note that the Holy Spirit and the Holy Ghost is one and the same. The Third Person of the Godhead/Trinity.

II. Peter Preaches by The Power of The Holy Ghost

As the disciples spoke strange languages, many who gathered to hear them were amazed, for they each heard them speak in his own language. So, they wondered what was happening, but some took to mockery of the disciples. So Peter, (by the power of the Holy Ghost), stood up to preach (his first sermon) to the people. He made them realize that the disciples did not speak out of drunkenness, but that what they were witnessing was the fulfillment of Joel’s prophecy (Joel 2:28-32): “And it shall come to pass afterward, that I will pour out My Spirit on all flesh and your sons and your daughters shall prophesy, your old men shall dream dreams, your young men shall see visions. And also upon the servants and upon the handmaids in those days will I pour out My Spirit…And it shall come to pass that whosoever shall call on the name of the lord shall be saved…” Acts 2:37-41 tell us that the people were pricked in their heart (having been convicted by the Holy Ghost) and they yielded to Peter’s alter call. The record is that on that same Pentecost day, about three thousand people were saved!
