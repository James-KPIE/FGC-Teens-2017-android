What Does The Holy Spirit Do?

Text: John 14-16

Memory Verse: “But the Counselor, the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in my name, will teach you all things and will remind you of everything I have said to you.” - John 14:26

Bottom Line: The Holy Spirit helps us with everything with which we might need help.

Lesson Objective: In this lesson teens will learn that the Holy Spirit is our Helper, Comforter, Teacher and Friend.

Lesson Introduction

Everyone is conscious of God whether or not they admit it. The Spirit applies the truths of God to minds of people to convince them that they are sinners. Responding to that conviction brings people to salvation. Once we are saved and belong to God, the Spirit takes up residence in our hearts, sealing us with the confirming, certifying, and assuring pledge of our eternal state as His children. Jesus said He would send the Spirit to us to be our Helper, Comforter, and Guide (Jn. 14:16; Rom.8:9; 1 Cor. 6:19-20, 12:13 ). The Greek word translated here “Counselor” means “one who is called alongside” and has the idea of someone who encourages and exhorts. Jesus gave the Spirit as a compensation for His absence, to perform the functions towards us which He would have done if He had remained personally with us.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The Holy Spirit does not only minister in the capacity of representing the Father and the Son. He also has a personal, distinct ministry of His own. We know that the Holy Spirit has had a personal ministry through the ages, yet it can be said that His personal ministry in this age unlike in the Old Testament times, has its separate and positive distinctions. Of the many names of the Holy Spirit used in the Bible, there are 90 distinct references to the Holy Spirit in the Old Testament alone! This lesson explains four names and ministries of the Holy Spirit that relate to teens. Those names and works are: Our Helper, Our Comforter, Our Teacher and Our Friend.

Lesson Outline

I. The Holy Spirit is our Helper (John 14:16)

II. The Holy Spirit as our Comforter (John 14:26)

III. The Holy Spirit as our Teacher (John 14:26)

IV. The Holy Spirit As our Friend (John 14:26)

Lesson Commentary

I. The Holy Spirit is our Helper

We need the Holy Spirit's help more than we know. How exactly does the Holy Spirit help us? Sometimes, we refer to the Holy Spirit as some vague spirit being that just hovers around to lead us this way and that. But Jesus is very clear: The Spirit is so much more. He is our real and necessary Helper and Advocate, sent to assist us in living the Christian life. Here are a few ways that the Holy Spirit helps us both for our good and God’s glory:

A. The Holy Spirit helps us pray when we don't know how (Rom.8:26).

We may think we know how to pray, and sometimes we do, but many times we don't have a clue what answer to prayer we really need. If you are praying wrongly, you won't get the right answers, or perhaps any answer. If you are not getting your prayer answered, consider James 4:3.

B. The Holy Spirit helps us pray God's perfect will (Rom. 8:27). The Holy Spirit has the mind of God. He always knows the perfect will of the Father and always gets His prayers answered (1 John 5:14-15). So if we know that He hears whatever we ask, we know that we have whatever we asked of Him.

II. The Holy Spirit as our Comforter

The word "comforter" is frequently used by Greek writers to denote an advocate in a court; one who intercedes; a monitor, a teacher, an assistant, a helper. It is somewhat difficult, therefore, to fix the precise meaning of the word. It may be translated as advocate, monitor, teacher, or helper. We learn particularly from the accounts that our Saviour gives of His work that His office was:

A. To comfort the disciples; to be with them in His absence and to supply His peace; and this is properly expressed by the word Comforter.

B. To aid them in their work; to advocate their cause, or to assist them in advocating the cause of religion in the world, and in bringing sinners to repentance; and this may be expressed by the word advocate (Jn. 16:7-13).

C. It was also by the Spirit that they were enabled to stand before kings and magistrates, and boldly to speak in the name of Jesus (Matt.10:20).

These seem to comprise all the meanings of the word in the New Testament. He shall be your constant guide and attendant. So, being our Comforter encompasses all the ministries of the Holy Spirit.

III. The Holy Spirit as our Teacher

References: Neh. 9:20; Matt. 10:19-20; Mk.13:11; Lk.12:12; I Jn.2:7. Our Lord Jesus in His last conversation with His disciples before His crucifixion said that the Comforter which is the Holy Ghost, shall teach us all things, and bring all things to our remembrance, whatsoever He had told us (John14:26).

Here we have a twofold work of the Holy Spirit, teaching and bringing to remembrance the things which Christ had already taught. We will take them in the reverse order.

A. The Holy Spirit brings to remembrance the words of Christ.

This promise was made primarily to the Apostles and is the guarantee of the accuracy of their report of what Jesus said. But the Holy Spirit does a similar work with each believer who expects it of Him, and who looks to Him to do it. The Holy Spirit brings to our mind the teachings of Christ and of the Word just when we need them for either the necessities of our life or of our service.

B. The Holy Spirit will teach us all things

It is the privilege of each believer in Jesus Christ, even the humblest, to be "taught of God." We have a Divine Teacher, the Holy Spirit. We shall never truly know the truth until we are thus taught directly by the Holy Spirit. No amount of mere human teaching, no matter who our teachers may be, will ever give us a correct and exact and full apprehension of the truth. Not even a diligent study of the Word either in the English or in the original languages will give us a real understanding of the truth. We must be taught directly by the Holy Spirit and we may be thus taught, each one of us. The Spirit will guide the one whom He thus teaches "into all the truth." There are two special lines of the Spirit's teaching mentioned:

1. "He shall declare unto you the things that are to come."

2. “He shall take of Mine and shall declare it unto you.”

This is the Holy Spirit's special line of teaching with the believer, as with the unbeliever, Jesus Christ.

IV. The Holy Spirit as our Friend

A. The Holy Spirit reveals to us the deep things of God which are hidden from and are foolishness to the natural man (1 Cor. 2: 9-13). The Holy Spirit takes the deep things of God which He hath prepared for us, even in the life that now is, and reveals them to us.

B. Not only is the Holy Spirit the Author of revelation, the written Word of God: He is also the Interpreter of what He has revealed (1 Cor. 3:14).

C. The Holy Spirit will help you find the right words to say in difficult situations (Luke 12:11-12).

2. What is He going to be like? What should I expect of Him?

3. What does “living with you” concept mean?
