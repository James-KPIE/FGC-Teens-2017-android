2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 33

God Rescues Peter

Lesson Starts

Text: Acts 12:1-24

Memory Verse: “Then Peter came to himself and said, "Now I know without a doubt that the Lord sent his angel and rescued me from Herod's clutches and from everything the Jewish people were anticipating." - Acts 12:11

Bottom Line: No chains can stop God’s deliverance.

Lesson Objective: This lesson will give teens greater assurance that Jesus can deliver from any bondage.

Lesson Introduction

The devil has proved to be the archenemy of Christians and the church of Jesus Christ. The early church faced different levels of persecution. There was a cold hearted enmity in Herod and the Jews against the church. This kind of bitterness is very much current in the world today including our immediate society. Christians and missionaries are facing serious persecutions all over the world. Islamic fundamentalists are committing professing Christians to death. Boko Haram in the North East Nigeria is a clear example. However, the scripture declared, “Who shall separate us from the love of Christ? Shall tribulation, or distress, or persecution, or famine, or nakedness, or peril, or sword? … Yet in all these things we are more than conquerors through Him who loved us” (Rom 8:35-37).

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The Jews were greatly opposed to the disciples of Jesus. Herod who was the Roman ruler at the time was Agrippa I. He was popular with the Jews for he was partly Jewish, being of Hasmonean descent. His kingdom covered basically the same area as that of his grandfather Herod the Great. He was doing everything possible to get the favour of the Jews. So, he found it politically expedient to arrest Christians. He executed James, the brother of John. He went further to arrest Peter to gain favour from the Jews. Herod Agrippa I died in A.D. 44. His son, Herod Agrippa II, was king of Judea from A.D. 50-70. Paul was on trial before Agrippa II and his sister Bernice (Acts 25:13-26:32).

Lesson Outline

I. Violence Against The Church (Acts 12:1-4)

II. Miraculous Escape of Peter (Acts 12:5-12)

III. The Brethren Received Peter (Acts 12:13-19)

IV. Violent Death of Herod (Acts 12:20-24)

Lesson Commentary

I. Violence Against The Church

The heavy persecution against the early church led to the execution of Apostle James. When King Herod saw that it was pleasing to the Jews, he also arrested Apostle Peter during the Feast of Unleavened Bread. This incident clearly indicates that the church was an “identifiable group" which had become hated and despised by the Jews. Herod intended to bring out Peter for public trial after the Passover. Herod made certain that Peter's imprisonment was secure by handing him over to be guarded by four squads of four soldiers each!

II. Miraculous Escape of Peter

The church gathered and was earnestly praying to God for him. Peter’s faith in the Lord was so strong that he was sound asleep the night before his trial (cf. 1 Pet. 2:23; 5:7). He did not fear for his life because Christ had said he would keep His own (John 21:18). An angel appeared in the prison and told him to get dressed and follow him out of the prison. Supernaturally, God caused the chains to fall off his wrists, kept the guards asleep, and opened the iron gate. When Peter came to himself, braced by the night air, he acknowledged God's deliverance for him from Herod and the Jews.

III. The Brethren Received Peter

The unexpected arrival of Peter at John Mark's home is filled with humour and human interest. The servant girl, Rhoda, who answered Peter's knock recognized his voice. Though the saints were praying earnestly for Peter's release, they did not expect an answer so soon! When Rhoda insisted, ‘Peter is at the door!’ They replied, ‘You're out of your mind. It must be his angel.’ When they saw Peter, they were astonished. After making himself known to the brothers, Peter left for another place. After an investigation of Peter's escape, Herod cross-examined the guards and ruthlessly ordered their executions. Herod must have lost about 16 guards by his actions.

IV. Violent Death Of Herod

Tyre and Sidon were in Herod's dominion and for some reasons had incurred his wrath. Because these cities depended on Galilee for sustenance, they desired to make peace with Herod Agrippa. On the appointed day when Herod was delivering a speech, the people honoured him as a god, and the Lord God judged him with death. This was in A.D. 44. After Herod's death, Felix and Festus, successively, were the governors of Judea. After the death of Herod Agrippa the Word of God continued to increase and spread. In spite of opposition and persecution the Lord sovereignly prospered the work of His church.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. The Church of Jesus Christ has been a focus for satanic persecution. Discuss.

2. Have you ever faced persecution in your Christian race? Narrate your experience.

3. What is the significance of Peter’s miraculous escape from the conspiracy of Herod and the Jewish people?

Lesson Summary

1. The persecution against the early church led to the arrest of Apostle Peter by King Herod who desired to please the Jews. While Apostle Peter was in prison, the angel of God showed up and rescued him.

2. Regardless of whatever we might pass through in the course of preaching the Gospel and in our Christian race, God is ever ready to save and deliver us. We should remember the words of Jesus, “These things I have spoken unto you, that in me ye might have peace. In the world ye shall have tribulation: but be of good cheer; I have overcome the world” (John 16:33). The Master concluded, “…lo, I am with you always, even unto the end of the world. Amen” (Matt 28:20).

3. We must remember in prayer other Christians who are being persecuted in the north of Nigeria, Egypt, Iraq, Syria, Sudan, China and other places. Pray for strength and preservation and they keep raising the banner for Christ.
