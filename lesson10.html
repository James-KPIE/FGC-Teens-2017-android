2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 10

Symbols of the Holy Spirit

Lesson Starts

Text: Acts 2:3; Matthew 3:16; Luke 3:22; John 7:38-39; Acts 2:1-2; and John 3:8.

Memory Verse: “When the day of Pentecost came, they were all together in one place. Suddenly a sound like the blowing of a violent wind came from heaven and filled the whole house where they were sitting.” - Acts 2:1-2

Bottom Line : We can see the character of the Holy Spirit through wind, fire, water, a dove and oil.

Lesson Objective : In this lesson teens will see a connection between the characteristics of God, the Holy Spirit, and various symbols used in the Bible to describe Him and He does in the life of Christians.

Lesson Introduction

The Holy Spirit is the third person in the Godhead. He is a person just like the Father and the Son. He revealed Himself in the scripture in various ways. Every believer must have an encounter with the Holy Spirit in any of the ways in which He has used in various places to reveal Himself. Just as the Father revealed Himself to the people of old through various ways, and the Son also revealed Himself in the flesh, so also the Holy Spirit has ways of revealing Himself to believers.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

The Lord Jesus Christ introduced the person of the Holy Spirit to His disciples in the gospel of John 14. He introduced the Holy Spirit to them as the "Counselor" who will be with them forever. He told them that the Holy Spirit will live in them. He told them that the Holy Spirit will teach them all things and will remind them of everything he told them. On the day of the baptism of Jesus Christ at River Jordan, John saw the Spirit descend on Jesus Christ in bodily form like a dove. Jesus Christ further described the presence of the Holy Spirit like water or river that flows continuously in the person that receives the baptism of the Holy Spirit. The Holy Spirit was also described as the oil which makes the work of grace upon any believer very easy.

Lesson Outline

I. The Holy Spirit As A Dove

II. The Holy Spirit As Wind

III. The Holy Spirit As River/Water

IV. The Holy Spirit As Fire

V. The Holy Spirit As Oil

Lesson Commentary

I. The Holy Spirit as a Dove

The Holy Spirit is depicted in symbolic form as a dove. At the baptism of Jesus Christ by John the Baptist at river Jordan, the Holy Spirit was seen descending upon Jesus Christ in bodily form (Luke 3:22, John 1:32, Mark 1:10, Matthew 3:16). The dove is an emblem of innocence or meekness and purity (Matt.10:6).

II. The Holy Spirit as Wind

On the day of Pentecost, the disciples were all together in one accord. The Scripture recorded that there came a sound from heaven as of a rushing mighty wind. Acts 2:2. The Spirit is likened to the wind in John 3:8. As it blows, so is the movement of the Holy Spirit. There’s a dynamism about the working of the Holy Spirit. You can contain or explain it, you just experience it. You can’t necessarily see where it came from or where it goes, but all of us can attest to times when God has come and dealt with us, and no human being knew how it happened. That is the way of the wind.

III. The Holy Spirit as River/Water

Jesus Christ pointed out that those who believe in Him, out of their belly shall flow rivers of living water (John 7:38-39). The Spirit is likened to an ever flowing water that any one that is thirsty can come and drink (Rev. 22:1, 17).

IV. The Holy Spirit as Fire

The Holy Spirit is likened to fire. The believer is admonished not to quench the fire of the Holy Spirit (1 Thess. 5:18). On the day of Pentecost, the Holy Spirit was depicted as cloven tongues of fire which sat on all in the upper room (Acts 2:3). Fire is a depiction of God’s presence, passion and purity.

V. The Holy Spirit as Oil

The Holy Spirit is likened to oil in the scripture. It is the enabling oil that makes ministration seem seamless. The presence of the Holy Spirit in any man is likened to anointing. Oil is the substance used during anointing processes. Oil makes the fire in a lamp to continue to glow and burn so is the presence of the Holy Spirit in a believer's life, it makes him to burn and glow for the Lord (Exo. 29:7, Acts 10:38).

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. What is the significance of the various symbols of the Holy Spirit in a believer’s life?

2. Which of the symbols do you relate with most and why?

3. Can the fire of the Holy Spirit be quenched in the life of a believer?

Lesson Summary

  1. The Holy Spirit is invisible, while our realm is visible. In each of the symbols discussed above, the Lord desires to move into our realm.
  1. The Holy Spirit comes to glorify Jesus—helping us to see Jesus more, to understand Jesus better, to respond to Jesus more obediently, and to love Jesus with a deeper heart of commitment, as He manifest through these symbols.
  1. So, as Jack Hayford writes, “the symbols of the Holy Spirit become essential to our gaining an understanding of what He’s like, not only in an objective way of analyzing truth, but also in the subjective way that He comes to penetrate our lives—ways in which the reality of the invisible penetrate the visible.”
