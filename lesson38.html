2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 38

A Call for Repentance

Lesson Starts

Text : Ezekiel 18:1-32

Memory Verse : "But if a wicked man turns away from all the sins he has committed and keeps all my decrees and does what is just and right, he will surely live; he will not die.” - Ezekiel 18:21

Bottom line : Repentance is the key to receiving justification.

Lesson Objective : At the end of this lesson, the teens will acknowledge that no one is beyond redemption.

Lesson Introduction

Seven years ago, the Nigerian government under President Umar Musa Yar’adua started the amnesty program for militants in Niger Delta region of Nigeria. Amnesty meant that the sin of sabotaging the economy was forgiven, and so militants, in realizing their folly and surrendering their arms, will be able to live a responsible and better life. Amnesty also meant that militants will get paid, even get trained or schooled as the case may be. In July 29, 2016 three out of the former militants graduated from a London University with First Class Honors, while 18 graduated with Second Class Upper division. This goes to show what can happen when people seize the opportunity to turn from being bad to good.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

In the days of prophet Ezekiel, the people of Judah gave themselves up to reckless abandon in sin. God dealt with them by allowing other countries invade and rule over them and even take them captives. The climax was the invasion of and eventually captivity by Babylon. Instead of them to repent, they became bitter, conceited and rebellious. They coined a proverb that denied God’s justice, and generalized judgement by nullifying God’s fairness in dealing with individuals. Living in denial of the repercussions of their sins, they played the victim, assuming they were being punished for the sins of their ancestors. God was however set to expose their hypocrisy, in overtly doling out judgement as they deserved.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. How does verse 20 of our text address the notion of those who think they are saved because they were born into a Christian family?

2. Why do you think it is difficult for some people to change from their wrong doing?

3. What do you make of Isaiah 1:18 in the light of today’s lesson?

Lesson Summary

If a human being could grant a fellow human being amnesty, how much more God who has the inalienable capacity to forgive sins. It is in God’s nature to forgive sins. For God was in Christ, reconciling the world to himself, no longer counting people's sins against them (2 Cor. 5:19). God’s appeal for repentance still stands today. It doesn’t matter how deep in sin you might fallen, it doesn’t matter all the bad things you might have done, you just need to come back to God and repent of your sins. For God made Christ, who never sinned, to be the offering for our sin, so that we could be made right with God through Christ (2 Cor. 5:21). The point is that the provision for our justification is a done deal in Christ. We only need to take advantage of God’s gracious call to repentance. I don't want you to die, says the Sovereign Lord. Turn back and live! (Ezek. 18:32)
