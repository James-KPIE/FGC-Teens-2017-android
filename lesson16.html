2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 16

Resurrection Faith (Easter Sunday)

Lesson Starts

Text: Mark 16:1-8

Memory Verse: "Don't be alarmed," he said. "You are looking for Jesus the Nazarene, who was crucified. He has risen! He is not here. See the place where they laid him.” - Mark 16:6

Bottom Line: Jesus’ resurrection is the greatest event in human history.

Lesson Objective: This lesson will help teenagers meaningfully connect their faith with the resurrection event.

Lesson Introduction

An atheistic lawyer named Frank Morrison attempted a research on Jesus’ resurrection with the intention to write a book disproving it. Instead, his discovery convinced him that it had happened. The experience led to his conversion and subsequently he wrote the book, Who Moved The Stone?, proving that Jesus’ resurrection actually took place. Cynics like Morrison had always existed from the first century when Jesus’ resurrection took place.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

To the Jewish leaders, the Resurrection story was a mere fraud concocted by Jesus to immortalize Himself in the minds of His disciples. They chose to be blind to the prediction concerning this event (Psalm.16:10) and staunchly vowed to foil any attempt by anyone to take Christ’s body from the tomb and announce to the people that he had risen. They rolled a huge stone over the entrance to the tomb and positioned soldiers to take down anyone who would approach the tomb to take the body (Matt. 27:62-66). It did not enter into their minds that God’s redemptive work is a divine arrangement unstoppable by any man and even the devil. It was not just the Pharisees and Jewish authority that disbelieved the possible resurrection of Jesus Christ on the third day after his death and burial. Even his own disciples – all of them, Peter inclusive, did not believe the resurrection story when the women who had gone to the tomb early Sunday morning, returned to disclose this historic event. They only believed when they saw the risen Jesus with their own eyes.

Lesson Commentary

I. Love in Despair

Mary Magdalene and Mary, the mother of James and Salome (women who followed Jesus) prepared sweet spices and went early on Sunday morning (the third day, counting from Friday when he died) to anoint his body. This was a customary practice that should have been done before burial. However because the body of Christ was brought down from the cross late Friday and the next day being Sabbath, the anointing had to be postponed. It is significant that the women brought spices for the anointing, as only the bodies of dignitaries like kings enjoyed such luxury. This was an indication of how much the women esteemed and loved Jesus. However, their action on this particular Sunday morning was a glaring display of either ignorance, forgetfulness or lack of understanding of what Jesus had said about his suffering in the hands of the Jewish religious rulers, and his death and resurrection on the third day (Matt. 16:21; 17:23; Mark. 8:31; 9:31; Luke. 9:22). Although the women may have loved Jesus and were loyal to him even in death, it appeared they did not believe his resurrection ‘sermon’. No doubt, any hope that Christ’s teaching ignited in them died with his death. ‘It is all over’ they seemed to have concluded.

II. Hope in Dismay

As they approached the tomb in which Jesus was buried, the women wondered, who would help them in rolling away the stone that covered the tomb. It would take more than 20 hefty men to roll away the stone. But thank goodness, when they got there, the stone had been rolled back. They were however caught by surprise when they got into the tomb and the body of Jesus wasn’t there. Instead, a young man clad in white (definitely an angel) told them not to be afraid and gave them the news that sent them into a deeper state of shock, “He has risen! He is not here.” They must have gone blank for a moment, trying to process what they just heard and possibly may have barely heard the angel tell them to go and announce the big news to the other disciples. With a mixture of fear and perplexity, they hurried out of the place, saying nothing to anyone. No doubt, a ray of hope was rising even in their bemusement. “He is risen.” That today would sound like a major news headline on the dailies or news channel, or the title of a new movie showing at the cinemas. Indeed, more than a news headline or a movie title, those three words were the game changer for the Christian faith.

Lesson Summary

It is the physical resurrection of Christ that sets apart Christianity from all other religions. Without Jesus’ resurrection, He would only remain in our minds as another legend in history. We wouldn’t be talking about salvation or the hope of resurrection in the last day. Jesus’ resurrection makes Him the author and finisher of faith (Heb. 12:2). His resurrection is also the foundation of all other resurrections. This makes the Christian faith a living faith and not a mythical one. Christ’s resurrection was part of the full package of redemption that God worked for humankind before the foundation of the earth (1.Peter.1:19-21). If the package was short of resurrection, but merely ended with the death of Christ, then humankind would have been doomed forever. All hope of humanity’s regeneration and reconciliation with God would have been lost. The confession of our faith (Rom 10:9) and the New Life we now share with Christ is based on Jesus’ resurrection (Col 3:1).

Discussion Questions/Activities

1 Why was it necessary that Jesus Christ should resurrect from the dead?

2 What does Christ’s resurrection assure us of? (Acts17:31; Jn.5:28-29).

3 What give us access to share in the resurrected life of Jesus? (Col. 2:12)
