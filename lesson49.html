2017 Teens Sunday School Manual

Lesson 49

Jesus’ Prayer for His Disciples

Lesson Starts

Text: John 17:1-26

Memory Verse: "Father, I want those you have given me to be with me where I am, and to see my glory, the glory you have given me because you loved me before the creation of the world.” - John 17:24

Bottom Line: Jesus intercessions make the difference in our Christian journey.

Lesson Objective: At the end of this lesson teens will have the confidence that they are not alone in the Christian journey.

Lesson Introduction

It is very common in Africa for aged parents who are about to die to gather and pray for their children and give them some specific instructions they must follow after their demise. Children in this position don’t take lightly the word of their dying parents. While they may be sorrowful when their parents eventually depart, they will still be happy that their parents blessed them before dying. Certainly, such valedictory blessings pronounced by parents in that scenario lingers long in the lives of the children. In the same vein, in our text, Jesus, nearing His departure, gathered His disciples together and committed them to the Father in Prayer.

Lesson Insight/Biblical Background

In the New Testament, Chapters 14-17 of the Gospel of John are known as the Farewell Discourse given by Jesus to his disciples, the night before He was crucified. The farewell message was more like the last words of a departing father to his children. This valedictory speech is classified into three categories. First, Jesus announces his departure to be with the Father— which invariably portends his suffering, crucifixion and resurrection and ascension. He however promises not to leave them comfortless. He will send the Holy Spirit to be with and guide them. Jesus commanded the disciples to love one another, emulating the unity and love in the Trinity. Second, Jesus illustrate the relationship the disciples have with Him and the Father, with the allegory of The Vine. The Vine again emphasizes the love among the disciples and their connection to Him, but Jesus then warns the disciples of upcoming persecutions, be He encourages them to find solace in Him, because, “I have overcome the world” ( John 16:33). In the final part of the discourse (John 17:1-26) Jesus prays for his followers and the coming Church. This is the longest prayer of Jesus in any of the gospels, and is known as the Farewell Prayer or the High Priestly Prayer. The key themes of the prayer are the glorification of the Father and the Son and petitions for the unity of the disciples through love.

Lesson Outline

I. Prayer For Himself (John 17:1-5)

II. Prayer For His Disciples (John 17:6-19)

III. Prayer For All Believers (John 17:20-26)

Lesson Commentary

I. Prayer for Himself

The time had come for Jesus to fulfill the purpose of God that was determined before the world was created (Rev 3:8) which is death by crucifixion for the salvation of humankind. All prophecy concerning the plan of redemption was now to be completed in Jesus (Lk 24:44). Therefore Jesus prayed to the father to glorify Him—the glorification of Jesus is linked to the threefold process of His crucifixion, resurrection and ascension. His act of obedience in surrendering to the cross allows for the completion of the work of the Christ beyond that event. He Himself had glorified the father by revealing the Father to disciples and by extension the Church (v. 6). Eternal life is made possible and given to all who know the Father through the Son. As Jesus is glorified, the lost world is redeemed and the Father is ultimately made known and glorified.

II. Prayer for His Disciples

From the prayer for Himself, Jesus moves to pray for His disciples. All this while Jesus had been with been with them as a shield against the world and the evil one. Now that he was leaving, He prayed the Father not to take them out of the world, because they would have to carry on with His work, but that they be preserved from the influences of sin and Satan so that they will not be polluted by the things of the world and so that they would be united as the Father and the Son (vv.11-12). Jesus further prayed that they would be sanctified (set apart) by the truth of God’s word (v.17). In being sanctified, the disciples would be able to live in obedience to God’s word, exude the character of Christ as they are shaped and directed by the Word of God. This way the disciples would fulfil the joy of Jesus in the sense that they would continue the redemptive work by spreading the Gospel to the world, invariably bring glory to Christ and the Father.

III. Prayer for All Believers

Having prayed for His immediate disciples, Jesus visualized those who were beyond His immediate company. He prayed for all those who would in the future become His disciples (v. 20), as the Gospel continues to spread. Interestingly, this is where you belong. Again, just like He did for the disciples, Jesus prays for the unity of all believers (v.21). The purpose of this unity is at least twofold: (1) that believers may be in the Father and the Son and (2) that the world may believe that the Father sent Jesus. Jesus prays that we may have the same oneness that He and the Father have. As we experience this God-like intimacy with one another, the world will believe that the Father sent the Son. In other words, Christian unity makes possible the expansion of God’s kingdom as more people are drawn to Christ. This again brings glory to Christ and God the Father. Rounding up, Jesus prays that believers would share in His glory (v. 24). Jesus actually securing the eternal destiny of believers. The grand design of Christ is, that all who believe should love and obey, persevere unto the end, and be eternally united to Him and the Father, in the kingdom of glory.

Discussion Questions/Activities

1. Compare and contrast Jesus’ prayer points with some of our payer points in our homes/churches today.

2. How do you feel that Jesus had you in mind and prayed for you while He was here on earth?

3. How do you think you can be an answer to Jesus’ prayer?

Lesson Summary

1. Jesus’ prayer for us is a summary of purpose for our lives as Christians. We are a product and part of His redemptive work on earth, we are also partakers of His glory as we have share an eternal destiny with Him.

2. We are to reflect the glory of the Son in our lives as we go about God’s ministry of restoration. We do this as we daily live our lives in obedience to His word and conformity to His character (Rom. 12:1-2} and by being God’s agents of restoration and transformation.

3. Jesus’ prayer is instructive of what should be the focus of our prayers. Our prayers should about moving the Kingdom forward. Prayers that move the Kingdom forward are those that focus on seeing: the lost saved and lives drawn to Christ, believers preserved as they grow in the truth of God’s word and Godlike unity, believers’ eternal destiny as they are secured to experience and enjoy His redemptive glory.
